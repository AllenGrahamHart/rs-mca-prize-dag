# e22_dyadic_minimal_scale_selector

- **status:** PROVED
- **closure:** proof

## Statement

For dyadic domains `n=2^m`, the quotient moduli are the powers of two and form
a finite chain under divisibility and size. Therefore every canonical
support-scale class from `e22_cross_scale_support_canonical_form` with at
least one admissible quotient modulus has a unique minimal admissible modulus.

Selecting that minimal modulus and its recovered tail/full-fiber data gives a
canonical representative of the cross-scale class.

## Falsifier

A dyadic canonical support-scale class with two distinct minimal admissible
moduli, or whose selected representative depends on a noncanonical choice.

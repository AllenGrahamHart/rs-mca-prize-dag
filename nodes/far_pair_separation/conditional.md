# far_pair_separation conditional proof

- **status:** CONDITIONAL
- **closure:** proof from predicate nodes

## Predicate nodes

## Claim

Conditional on the predicate nodes, there is a class family `F` of size
`> B*/poly` whose pairwise `e1`-differences are certified by only polynomially
many per-row generator checks.

## Proof

Assume `generator_economy`. It supplies cluster-center configurations of the
required size scale, with every center difference lying in

```text
(height-budget unit) * (multiplicative semigroup generated by g <= poly bases).
```

The admissibility guard in that node keeps the construction independent of
fiber-count information.

Now apply `cluster_certificates`.

First, each diameter-`d*` cluster is a free clique: all internal pairs are
certified by the graded collision-radius rule.

Second, for two clusters with center difference `Delta`, every cross-pair
difference has the form

```text
Delta + eta = Delta * (1 + Delta^(-1) eta),
```

where `eta` is the bounded cluster perturbation. The everywhere-big margin in
`cluster_certificates` puts the quotient factor below the remaining norm
budget, so a single norm/divisibility check on `Delta` certifies all cross
pairs between those two clusters.

Third, because each `Delta` factors through the polynomial base set from
`generator_economy`, divisibility distributes over the product. Checking the
polynomially many base generators therefore certifies all center differences.
The height-budget unit and the integer freebies are harmless under the
`cluster_certificates` integer-factor clause.

Thus the union of the clusters gives the required all-pairs-distinct family:
internal pairs are free, cross-pairs reduce to center checks, and all center
checks reduce to the polynomial generator list.

The negative `height_only_impossibility` node explains why this factorized
route is necessary at official scales; it is not an additional hypothesis.
The only remaining open mathematical content is the center-design existence
asserted by `generator_economy`.

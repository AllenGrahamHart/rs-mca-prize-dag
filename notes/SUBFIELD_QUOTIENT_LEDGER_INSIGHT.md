# The unifying insight: five reds are faces of ONE dichotomy (2026-07-05)

Five of the six parallel Pro windows (P1-P5) returned as (C)-conditionals, and —
strikingly — every one reduces its node to the SAME structural dichotomy:

    each object is either  QUOTIENT/COSET/SUBFIELD/TRACE-structured (must be PAID)
                     or    a BOUNDED primitive residual (small, uncharged).

| red  | charged part (paid gate)              | bounded residual                  |
|------|---------------------------------------|-----------------------------------|
| P1 e22 | mu_M coset covariance H_i=X^e G(X^M) | non-covariant part in bounded tail|
| P2 dli | trace-flat / subfield frequencies     | fixed-freq Bohr flatness elsewhere|
| P3 sov | trace-flat / subfield-norm cells      | power-sum bound on residual cells |
| P4 m720| subfield-compressed conductor         | height-bounded norm != 0 mod p    |
| P5 petal| quotient/coset cofactor directions   | dim <= B_pet primitive residual   |

VERIFIED counterexamples that forced this: P2 (F_{p^2} trace-zero lambda,
S=Omega(L_j)); P3 (F_{17^32} canonical trace char, 496/512 trace-flat, density
0.506 at h=21); P1 (interpolation defeats every bounded tail).

## Two shared obligations behind all five
(a) A complete SUBFIELD/QUOTIENT/TRACE PAID-GATE LEDGER — must EXPLICITLY include
    a trace-flat/subfield-norm gate (P2, P3 showed this gate was missing and is
    NOT small — positive density). Covers the charged part of all five.
(b) A BOUNDED-PRIMITIVE-RESIDUAL theorem: off the paid gate, the residual (non-
    subfield, non-quotient, robustly aperiodic) part is bounded/cancelling.

## Strategic consequence
The six reds are not six independent research problems — they are five facets of
one subfield/quotient/trace ledger + one bounded-residual theorem (plus rate_half,
P6, still out). Proving (a)+(b) once, uniformly, would collapse the endgame.
This is the highest-value target the parallel-window experiment surfaced.

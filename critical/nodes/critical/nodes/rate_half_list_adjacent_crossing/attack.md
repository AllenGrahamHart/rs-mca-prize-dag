# Attack surface

The complete branches `B*=1,2` are closed at `a_L=3n/4` by the proved
low-budget exact-crossing theorem. On the remaining branches `B*>=3`, the
unsafe side at the prize-max razor row is already proved by
`rate_half_cyclic_rotated_prefix_floor`. The safe side now has the proved
exact-integer Johnson anchor `a_IJ(C)`. A closure must narrow the interval
between those two bounds until one candidate has both a safe proof and an
unsafe predecessor.

Selected routes:

1. strengthen the Johnson anchor with rate-half image-fiber/codegree structure
   while preserving its exact integer endpoint;
2. derive a direct profile-envelope theorem for the non-polynomial received
   words left outside the truncated-locator moment-map census;
3. move the safe point upward if necessary and pair the new upper theorem with
   a matching unsafe witness at its predecessor.

For the four quadratic budget-three chambers, the geometry is already reduced
to the primitive full-rank module

```text
(alpha,Xalpha,beta,Xbeta),
```

with coprime generators, exact degrees, and trivial intersection of the two
length-two Krylov spaces. Any further attack must price or exclude the
resulting four-moving-target subgroup factorization; rank collapse and a
hidden constant split-unit relation are no longer available routes.

On the maximal `n=2^41`, B*=3 row, split any arithmetic attack into the proved
field alternatives before invoking cyclotomic estimates: either `e=1` with
`p=1 mod 2^41`, or `e=2` with `p=+/-1 mod 2^40`. Smaller domains are outside
this field-degree reduction.

For the nine linear B*=3 chambers, discard the proved-impossible construction
in which every related locator is one degree-`d` quotient fiber with only its
exceptional roots removed. Partial fractions make those locators independent.
The surviving line-side attack must therefore use primitive blocks or a
genuinely multi-fiber quotient pattern.

An upper bound at an unrelated agreement is not a close. The Johnson anchor
is a rigorous safe bracket, but its failed test one step below is not an
unsafe witness.

The direct falsifier for a proposed remaining-branch certificate is one admissible rate-half
row and received word with more than `B*` codewords at its asserted safe
agreement. Numerical survival is evidence only.

The known `F_17` path pattern is no longer a candidate for direct transport:
its exact minor norm restricts the rank defect to characteristic `17`, and
that characteristic is incompatible with the prize-max domain and field cap.
Searches or constructions in the path chamber must change the exponent
assignment or the primitive split-unit coupling, rather than lift the printed
support pattern unchanged.

For cycle/path quotient constructions, do not continue the equal-fiber route
with fiber size at least four: the top locator coefficients already give a
Vandermonde contradiction. The honest residual is fiber size one or two,
different quotient maps across blocks, incomplete fibers, or primitive
locators. Any construction search should target one of those named forms.

Across all nine linear chambers, equal complete fibers of size at least eight
are excluded even with the exact one/two-root deletion profiles. The only
additional direct one-map scale is fiber size four in linear `K_4-e`, `K_4`,
and triangle-plus-singleton. Treating a larger equal-fiber construction as an
open search direction would duplicate a proved impossibility.

At fiber size four, linear `K_4-e` and triangle-plus-singleton are also
excluded. For linear `K_4`, attack only the rank-two reciprocal locus
`dim_(F(X^4))<1/P_i>=2`; rank three and four are proved impossible. The
antipodal component `P_i=X^2-a_i` is the first exact subcase to classify
against the official subgroup factorization.

For that antipodal component, work after the proved `Y=X^4` descent. The
remaining objects are four pairwise-coprime degree-`2^37-1` factors in a
two-dimensional base-field pencil whose product is the order-`2^39`
subgroup polynomial with four roots removed. Do not return to degree-`2^39`
locator matrices.

The complete `d=8` quotient boundary over `F_97` has `192` antipodal pencil
assignments with nonzero relation coefficients. They are Möbius-graph
configurations. Any official exclusion must therefore be scale-sensitive;
an unstructured larger boundary sweep is not a closure argument.

Use the proved Möbius weld: after a pencil basis change, the antipodal branch
is `product_i(R+a_iS)=kappa (Y^d-1)/product_i(Y-a_i^2)`. The next analytic
target is rigidity for this quartic norm equation. Treating the four pencil
parameters as independent would discard a proved constraint.

Do not route that residual back into a quotient-periodic construction. The
reduced welded map has odd degree `d/4-1`, excluding every nontrivial cyclic
or dihedral dyadic pullback, and the direct four-coset partition has
Vandermonde rank four. The remaining target is primitive nonperiodic
rigidity for the quartic norm equation.

Retain the proved reverse-contact degree floor. In the canonical monic pencil
`G_i=U+c_iV`, the lower-degree direction satisfies
`deg V>=2^36-2`. Constant-direction or other low-degree polynomial-composition
models are closed. Any coefficient or CAS attack should begin in this
high-degree interval rather than rediscovering the excluded translation
component.

Split off the centered pure-quartic stratum before attacking the generic
outer parameters. If `e_2=e_3=0`, the second-derivative Wronskian proves the
exact identity `deg V=r-1=2^37-2`; there is no wider degree interval in that
stratum. Its second-derivative Wronskian has only one nonzero linear residual
after removing `Y^(d-2)U^2V^2`; both directions are squarefree and together
meet the deleted divisor or zero at most once. The generic `e_2!=0` and
intermediate `e_2=0,e_3!=0` strata retain
reverse-contact floors of `2^36-2` and `(2^38-4)/3`, respectively.

In the pure stratum, apply the harmonic lift router before attacking the
degree-`r` factors. Normalize one lift to one, enumerate or classify only

```text
w=(2x-y(1+x))/(1+x-2y)
```

inside the order-`2d` subgroup, and reject any antipodal pair because the
squared deleted roots must be distinct. For every surviving harmonic orbit,
the exact polynomial target is the coprime equation `Q=B^4+Z^4` with
`B(0)=1`, `ord_0Z=1`, and degrees at most `r`. Keep the existing squarefree
and linear-Wronskian residual checks. Do not claim that harmonicity alone is
empty; complete small-order scans disprove that route.

At those two floor boundaries, impose the proved differential residual before
attempting a symbolic classification:

```text
T=dDU-Y(D'U+4DU'),
deg T=1  (generic boundary),
deg T=2  (intermediate boundary).
```

It follows in particular that `U` has at most one or two roots, respectively,
that are repeated or meet `Z(D) union {0}`. A CAS decomposition that ignores
this low-degree residual is solving a substantially larger problem than the
actual frontier. Above the floors, stratify by the exact residual degree
`r+4-q(r-deg V)` rather than treating all high-degree directions together.

For the linear and quadratic boundary residuals, eliminate `U` completely.
With `E=z^4D(z^-1)`, compute the normalized formal series
`E^(-1/4)=sum a_mz^m`; the only possible monic `U` is the reverse of its
degree-`r` truncation. The generic boundary can exist only if

```text
a_(r+1)=a_(r+2)=0,       a_(r+3)!=0,
```

and the intermediate boundary only if

```text
a_(r+1)=0,               a_(r+2)!=0.
```

Attack these coefficient gaps over the actual four-deleted-root family
before launching a component decomposition with official-degree coefficients.
The four-term recurrence for `a_m` is exact, but an isolated point evaluation
does not cover the variable deleted-root family.

For the generic boundary, impose the nested secondary gap as well. Once the
canonical `B` is formed, compute

```text
J=z^(-2h)(E^(-1)(1-z^d)-B^4)/B^2,
P=(J/J(0))^(1/2).
```

The normalized reverse of `V` is `P mod z^h`, and its degree `h-3` forces
the coefficients of `P` at `2^36-1` and `2^36` to vanish. A useful generic
classification must attack the two primary fourth-root zeros and these two
secondary square-root zeros simultaneously; searching over free coefficients
of either `U` or `V` discards proved uniqueness.

Do not replace the simultaneous gap target by a primary-only nonvanishing
claim. The exact order-64 noncoset orbit `{0,1,3,-2}` has both required
primary zeros but fails only at the secondary coefficients `102,24` in
`F_193`. Since `193>64`, this rules out a shortcut under the generic
`char(F)>d` hypothesis. At `p=4289`, the first order-64 prime above the much
stronger `p>=d^2` threshold, the same pattern fails the primary gate. That
threshold is not available uniformly: the official maximal row splits into
`q=p` with `p>=3*2^128` and `q=p^2` with only `p>2^64`, at `d=2^39`.
Any primary-only repair must treat those arithmetic branches separately.

For the simultaneous gate, use the proved two-window reduction before
forming the nested square root. With `r=2h-3`, set

```text
L=sum_(m<h)a_mz^m,       T=sum_(m<h)a_(2h+m)z^m.
```

After the primary gap, the secondary condition is exactly

```text
L T=a_(2h)C^2 mod z^h,       C(0)=1,       deg C<=h-3.
```

The shifted tail obeys a first-order differential equation with linear
forcing. Attack this square-plus-differential system over the dyadic deleted
roots; do not allocate official-degree `B,C` coefficient arrays.

The order-128 pilot shows that the size-64 primary-gap orbit can consist of
two deleted antipodal pairs. Split that stratum algebraically: parity makes
one primary and one secondary coefficient automatic, after which the
remaining equations should be written in `w=z^2` and one normalized torsion
ratio. Do not extend the raw order sweep; prove the reduction and attack the
resulting univariate equations.

That reduction is now proved. With `d=16M`, compute `F_j(t)` by

```text
4jF_j=(4j-3)(1+t)F_(j-1)-(4j-6)tF_(j-2).
```

Attack the common-root system `F_(2M)(t)=G_M(t)=0` on
`t in mu_(8M)\{1}`, retaining `F_(2M+1)(t)!=0`. Prefer an exact recurrence,
Bezout, or torsion argument with exceptional-characteristic control. A long
resultant or fixed-`M` gcd table is only evidence and should not be scaled up.

If the univariate gate survives, immediately impose the canonical parity
factorization

```text
(1-w^(8M))/((1-w)(1-tw))
 =(B_0^2+lambda w^(2M+1)C_0^2)
  (B_0^2+mu     w^(2M+1)C_0^2).
```

Both factors have exact degree `4M-1` and partition the undeleted torsion
roots. Attack this as a two-cell primitive split-pencil census. Direct coset
partitions are already excluded; any classification must retain nonperiodic
cells and the Möbius matching. Equivalently, attack the signed root-cell
function whose Fourier transform vanishes at frequencies `0,...,2M`; its
first nonzero moment is fixed at `2M+1`, not merely bounded.

The primitive resultant already removes the prime-field and nonsplit
quadratic branches for a complete deleted-pair survivor. Restrict any further
square-pencil classification to `q=p^2`, `p=1 mod 2^40`. Do not spend compute
on the two eliminated field branches. Work over `F_p` for every quotient-
pencil coefficient, outer root, and Möbius parameter; retain `F_{p^2}` only
when the full order-`2^41` evaluation domain is actually used.

Before eliminating the surviving split-quadratic square pencil, reverse to
the half-degree original-coordinate system

```text
D=D_0(x),       U=YU_0(x),       V=V_0(x),       x=Y^2.
```

Do not allocate coefficients for `U_0`. The exact linear residual gives

```text
(16M-4)D_0U_0-2xD_0'U_0-8xD_0U_0'=kappa,
```

whose recurrence determines the unique possible monic `U_0` from `D_0` and
has one terminal equation. Impose that equation first. Preserve the forced
simple root `U(0)=0`; saturate away any second exceptional root, but not this
one. Then classify the simultaneous half-degree factorization

```text
D_0(x)(xU_0^2+lambda V_0^2)(xU_0^2+mu V_0^2)=x^(8M)-1.
```

Do not retain `q=mu/lambda` as a free scalar in that classification. Normalize
the deleted-root lifts to `(1,iota,r,iota r)`, with `r^4=t`, and shard only
the three exact Möbius branches

```text
r^2(1+q)^2=4q(r^2-r+1)^2,
(r-1)^4(1+q)^2=4q(r+1)^4,
(r^2+1)^2(1+q)^2=4q(r^2-4r+1)^2.
```

Each branch determines one unordered reciprocal ratio. The cleared router
must retain `q=-1`, but the official harmonic-exclusion theorem now removes
it completely after the router: two pairings have incompatible orders and
the third has no characteristic in the exact official interval. In every
remaining branch impose `q^N=1`, which follows from the two root-cell constant
terms; a general `F_p` ratio is not an admissible candidate.

There is no remaining reason to search coefficients of `V_0` or a common
outer scale. Form

```text
Q=(x^N-1)/D_0,       A=xU_0^2,       R=Q-A^2.
```

For each nonharmonic ratio, divide `R=AS+T` and test exactly

```text
deg S=2M-2,
T=qS^2/(1+q)^2,
S/(1+q) is a nonzero square.
```

No official `q=-1` shard remains. The nonharmonic conditions are necessary
and sufficient for reconstruction of the half-degree square pencil. A
prefix-square check or a sampled factorization is not sufficient.

Eliminate `q` before any contributor computation. For the three pairings put

```text
(a_0,b_0)=(r^2,(r^2-r+1)^2),
(a_1,b_1)=((r-1)^4,(r+1)^4),
(a_2,b_2)=((r^2+1)^2,(r^2-4r+1)^2).
```

The exact branch test is

```text
4b_jT=a_jS^2,       y=4b_j/a_j-2,
y notin {2,-2},       y_(m+1)=y_m^2-2,       y_38=2,
S/(1+q) is a nonzero square for X^2-yX+1=0.
```

The last verdict is independent of the reciprocal root chosen. A useful
uniform attack therefore proves that all three one-variable tests reject as
`r` ranges over the official torsion family. Do not allocate a separate
outer-ratio dimension or shard reciprocal roots.

Use the stronger trace/fourth-power form in an implementation. Put
`chi=r+r^(-1)` and

```text
h_0=1/(2(chi-1)),
h_1=(chi-2)/(2(chi+2)),
h_2=chi/(2(chi-4)).
```

The selected scalar equation is exactly `T=(h_jS)^2`. Conditional on it,
`S/(1+q)` is a square if and only if `T` is a nonzero fourth power of a
degree-`M-1` polynomial. Therefore the final contributor certifier should
check the cleared scalar identity, the 38-step trace, and one exact fourth-
power certificate. It should never construct `q` or run a root-dependent
square test.

Project to the constant coefficient before running that polynomial test. Let

```text
sigma=[z^(2M-2)](R_rev/A_rev mod z^(2M-1))=S(0).
```

The three branches require

```text
t sigma^2+4(chi-1)^2=0,
t(chi-2)^2sigma^2+4(chi+2)^2=0,
t chi^2sigma^2+4(chi-4)^2=0.
```

Attack a compressed recurrence or diagonal representation for this one
terminal coefficient. Do not allocate full `S,T` arrays merely to test these
equations.

Before extracting a fourth root, apply the differential gcd gate. Define

```text
P=2N+kappa x^2U_0^3.
```

Every passing branch must satisfy `S|P^2` and
`deg gcd(S,P)>=M-1`. Compute `P mod S`, square that residue modulo `S`, and
certify the gcd degree; do not form the dense polynomial `P^2`. A uniform
bound `deg gcd(S,P)<M-1` would close the entire nonharmonic deleted-pair
sublane without constructing `W`.

After the four gaps pass, run the full canonical span certifier rather than a
new coefficient search. Form `Rbar`, `Cbar`, and

```text
S=Rbar-alpha B^2Cbar^2,
X=z^hBCbar^3,
Y=z^(2h)Cbar^4.
```

Read `beta` at degree `h` and `gamma` at degree `2h`, then verify every
coefficient of `S-beta X-gamma Y` is zero. Finally check that the resulting
centered quartic splits into four distinct parameters with the Möbius matching
to square-root lifts of the deleted roots. Common scaling permits one deleted
root to be normalized to one, and permutations are redundant, but any
implementation must provide a coverage proof for those reductions.

For the intermediate floor, do not search over the degree-`2h-2` direction.
Form `H`, its normalized cube root `C_*`, and the two coefficients
`Delta,kappa`. Apply the exact gate

```text
3kappa-uDelta=0.
```

When `Delta` is nonzero, test only `u=3kappa/Delta`. When `Delta=0` and
`kappa!=0`, reject. When both vanish, retain one scalar explicitly and use
Hensel recursion rather than silently choosing `u`. Every survivor must pass
polynomiality, the exact multiplied identity, quartic splitting, and Möbius
matching. A long zero prefix of the formal solution is not polynomiality.

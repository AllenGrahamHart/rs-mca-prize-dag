# C36' cutoff-18 weighted excess

- **status:** see `dag.json` (single source of truth)
- **legacy id:** `f3_h3_mobius_excess_half`

For every official row, define

```text
P(t)=#{(a,b) in A^2: ab=t},
R(t)=#{(c,d) in A^2: d/c=t},
X_18=sum_(t!=1) max(P(t)-18,0) R(t).
```

Then

```text
17 X_18 <= 300 n^2.                              (WX18)
```

This is the exact remaining premise after the identity fiber and the first
`18` units of every nonidentity quotient fiber are paid. It is weaker than the
former cutoff-35 statement `X_35<=n^2/2`, which implies `(WX18)` by the proved
cutoff comparison.

The proved `f3_h3_rich_fiber_norm_cutoff` theorem gives `X_18=0` whenever
`p>6^(n/4)`. Thus the universal statement remains open only for
`n^2<=p<=6^(n/4)`.

For each fixed order, the proved low-distance ideal-star router gives a
sharper exact finite reduction inside that middle corridor. Every prime
supporting `P(t)>=19` divides the normalized ideal norm generated by two
shifted-product collisions with one common center and Parseval distance at
most six. This ideal norm divides the gcd of the two normalized principal
norms. Hence primes outside the finite rooted-star candidate union have
`X_18=Y_18=0`. The candidate ideal norms have not been completely classified
or factored at any official order. Once a complete relevant-prime list is
available, the proved prime-alignment criterion screens it without ideal
normal forms: one primitive `n`th root modulo each prime is enough to test the
complete rooted-star union.

Squared-distance-two collision norms are powers of two, so that class is
empty at every odd row prime. The selected seven small vectors in a rich fiber
therefore have at least six distance-at-most-six edges; only distance four and
six remain in the low-distance candidate generator.

The distance-four class is further routed. Every selected same-fiber edge is
either antipodal or has a cross signed-monomial overlap; the generic lane
reduces to `uv=-y` and `ux(1-y)=u^2-y`. Thus its fourth root is eliminated and
the collision is a two-free-variable subgroup incidence. The antipodal lane is
the second exact two-variable condition `x^2=u+v-uv`.
Their complete selected edge ledger is at most `(3n^2+n)/2`; consequently at
most `floor((3n^2+n)/12)` rich targets can avoid a distance-six edge.

The packing deficit has a stronger local form. Weight distance-four edges by
two and distance-six edges by one. Every selected seven-vector rich-fiber
graph has total weight at least eleven and hence a center of incident weight
at least four. The normalized collisions on a minimal set of two to four such
leaves generate a weighted-multistar ideal whose norm is divisible by the row
prime. At a candidate prime, one fixed primitive root screens this sharper
union by testing weighted degree at least four inside each product fiber.

The cutoff excess controls a stronger ladder. If `e=P(t)-18`, the rich fiber
has at least `7+ceil(e/2)` small vectors. Its weighted graph has a center of
degree `L(m)=m-4` for even `m` and `m-3` for odd `m`, at the guaranteed
`m=7+ceil(e/2)`. Thus every `e>=3` fiber has center weight at least six and
every `e>=7` fiber has weight at least eight. The only profiles that can avoid
weight six are `(P,D)=(19,1)` and `(20,2)`.

For every cutoff `0<=E<=17`, total quotient mass pays all excess at most `E`:

```text
X_18^<=E <= E(n-1)(n-2).
```

It is therefore enough to prove

```text
17X_18^>E <= 300n^2-17E(n-1)(n-2).
```

The Pareto cutoffs `E=0,2,6,10,14` require center weights
`4,6,8,10,12`, respectively. The selected high-degree interface is `E=14`:
only targets with `P>=33` remain, with exact numerator budget
`300n^2-238(n-1)(n-2)`.

For that tail, let `N_d(t)` count eligible unordered product-collision edges
of squared distance `d`. The exact centroid ratio is

```text
P(t)-18 <=(16/83)(2N_4(t)+N_6(t)).
```

Hence C36' follows from

```text
272 sum_(P(t)>=33)(2N_4(t)+N_6(t))R(t)
 <=83(300n^2-238(n-1)(n-2)).
```

After canonical orientation, each edge–quotient record is exactly a rational
four-variable/two-subgroup-membership incidence. The distance-four router
reduces its generic and antipodal lanes further to three variables with two
membership tests; distance six remains the four-variable bottleneck.

Distance four can be removed from the selected estimate. Inside one product
fiber, canonically orienting every generic distance-four edge gives indegree
at most one. Including the unique possible antipodal representation yields
`N_4(t)<=2(m-1)`. The unique antipodal vector has norm one rather than three,
which strengthens the centroid ledger on the worst branch. Together these
facts give, for every `P(t)>=33`,

```text
P(t)-18 <=(8/21)N_6(t).
```

Thus it is enough to prove the sole distance-six moment bound

```text
136 sum_(P(t)>=33)N_6(t)R(t)
 <=21(300n^2-238(n-1)(n-2)),
```

whose conservative right-side form is
`sum N_6(t)R(t)<(651/68)n^2<9.574n^2`.

The distance-six endpoint now splits by its unique possible antipodal
representation. On antipodal-free targets,

```text
P(t)-18 <=(16/53)N_6(t),
```

sharper than the uniform ratio by a factor `53/42`. On antipodal targets,
fixing the
canonical pair `{x,-x}` gives `t=1-x^2`; each edge–quotient record has four
free subgroup roots but three derived subgroup-membership tests. Therefore it
is enough to prove

```text
136(42M_6,33^0+53M_6,33^A)
 <=1113(300n^2-238(n-1)(n-2)).
```

Equivalently, the antipodal lane has relative weight `53/42`.
Conservatively, the weighted allowance is `(1643/136)n^2<12.081n^2`.

For an analytic incidence proof, two lower-cutoff interfaces remain exact
Pareto alternatives. With `B_(n,E)=300n^2-17E(n-1)(n-2)`, either

```text
4(10M_6,25^0+17M_6,25^A)<=5B_(n,6),
17(8M_6,29^0+11M_6,29^A)<=22B_(n,10)
```

also implies C36'. Their conservative allowances are respectively

```text
M_6,25^0+(17/10)M_6,25^A <=(99/4)n^2,
M_6,29^0+(11/8)M_6,29^A <=(715/34)n^2.
```

The `E=14` interface remains selected for fixed-order candidate generation
because it forces weighted multistar degree twelve. The `E=6` interface is
the largest-allowance analytic target proved here.

The support-overlap strata in that `E=6` interface are now paid. Every
overlapping generic distance-six edge belongs to one of two cubic one-
parameter covers, giving at most six such edges per target; an antipodal

```text

```

where `D_6,25^0,D_6,25^A` retain only generic--generic distance-six edges
whose six signed atoms are pairwise disjoint across the endpoints. This is

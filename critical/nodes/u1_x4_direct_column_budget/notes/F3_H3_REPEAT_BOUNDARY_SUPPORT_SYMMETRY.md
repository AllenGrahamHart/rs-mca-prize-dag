## Mobius Orbit

For a normalized boundary triple, write

```text
U=u-1,   V=v-1,   W=w-1,
UV+UW+VW=0,
r=U/V.
```

Then

```text
W/V = -r/(r+1).
```

Permuting the ordered triple `(u,v,w)` replaces `r` by one of

```text
r,
1/r,
-r/(r+1),
-(r+1)/r,
-(r+1),
-1/(r+1).
```

These are the six cross-ratio transforms generated by the `S_3` action on the
three centered entries.

The orbit collapses only in the already-excluded coefficient-collision cells

```text
r in {0, -1, 1, -1/2, -2}
```

or in the triple-repeat cell

```text
r^2+r+1=0,
```

which `F3_H3_REPEAT_BOUNDARY_Q0_CELL.md` pays separately.

Therefore the genuine non-q0 support is a union of six-element Mobius orbits.
If `R_orb` denotes the quotient support, then

```text
R_genuine = 6 R_orb.
```

This improves constants and gives the correct target for a future support
theorem, but it does not itself bound `R_orb`.

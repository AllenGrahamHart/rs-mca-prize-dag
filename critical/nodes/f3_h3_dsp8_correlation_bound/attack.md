# Attack surface

## Exact target and route hierarchy

The theorem-level falsifier is one official row violating `(DSP8)`. The
following cheaper events kill only sufficient routes:

1. `P(t)>=25` kills the vacuous `P<=24` satellite;
2. failure of the `(36,1)` class-sensitive moment kills that Pareto endpoint;
3. failure of `M_21<=76599n^2/680` kills only the unweighted factorial-moment
   route; and
4. a candidate prime or rich target kills no inequality by itself.

The preferred theorem attack is a Sidon-strip or smooth-trace argument that
proves the class-sensitive DSP8 moment directly. The preferred finite
fallback is the `(36,1)` endpoint because it has one quotient lift and no
quotient-collision spoke. The preferred vacuity fallback is the exact
`G_12^neq|H_D` certificate. These routes are alternatives and their
certificates must not be mixed.

Before any fixed-order campaign, try the exact paired-PGL2 rectangle theorem

```text
I_inv>=19  =>  I_aff<=18.
```

It is exactly `P>=19 => R<=17` and closes C36'. A convenient stronger scalar
certificate on the rich locus is the score cap

```text
I_inv+2I_aff<=56
```

This does not prove DSP8 itself, but it discharges the same consumer and has
seventeen more score units than the older global cap `39` posed for M35.
Threshold `57` admits `(P,R)=(19,18)`, so no larger integer score cap can
imply the rectangle. Since cap 56 over-solves profiles with `P>19`, prefer the
rectangle implication itself.

## Norm route

Every known C36 star or star-plus-coupling packet supplies one row prime ideal
and only the ceiling `6^(n/4)/4`. This cannot contradict `p>=n^2`. A new norm
proof must produce at least one of:

1. a growing family of distinct Galois prime ideals above `p`;
2. a common-ideal norm ceiling below `n^2`; or
3. a complete engineered-prime sieve.

More same-prime star generators, raw sparse-relation norms, and unpriced
resultants do not supply any of these inputs.

No official-scale Modal run is locally authorized. A useful implementation
must first reproduce the complete `n=8` Taylor packet, compare at least two
proved support methods on a complete small order, and exercise a
maximum-degree orbit block. At `n=8192`, the maximum-degree class contains
`12,285` of `24,534` blocks and `75.009%` of total algebraic degree; a pilot
using only small blocks is not a cost estimate.

Production is an external contributor request until a measured pilot supplies
a conservative CPU, RAM, time, storage, and dollar ceiling. Shards are keyed
by `(j,sign,w)`, balanced by algebraic degree, checkpointed independently,
and accepted only with the exact support/factor certificates and streaming
checker in CR-001. Partial output is evidence only.

Do not launch a raw quotient-orbit fleet, enumerate rooted product stars,
materialize the degree-`(n-1)^2` product table, or infer all-order coverage
from one fixed order.

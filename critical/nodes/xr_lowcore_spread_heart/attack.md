# Attack surface

The target is support-local, per pair, and globally generic. Any attack must
count distinct slopes in the selected pairwise-`(K-1)` family, not raw
supports, moments, or mean behavior.

The proved all-LineRay charge pays selector rank at most three. Cogirth and
affine-pencil tools apply under the target's generic hypothesis. Globally
nongeneric families and their canonical full-external-zero descent route to
P-A2, not this target.

The support indicators now have an exact shift-pair split. For an oriented
difference of width `t`, its multiplicity is a residual constant-weight code
with the same gap `H=h+1`. More sharply, every fiber with `t>=K` has
multiplicity at most one: two records would give two supports sharing its
fixed `t`-side against the cap `K-1`. This includes the complete terminal
Plotkin interval on all six rows. Therefore the Boolean additive-energy
attack should work only on the repeated-difference range

```text
H<=t<=K-1.
```

This does not force high energy and does not count slopes. It is not CAP25's
local locator-SPI currency. The next useful theorem is narrow-difference
control that retains the RS realization and
first-match color; another width-`t>=K` or terminal census would duplicate a
proved bound.

Every live repeated difference in that range now has an exact RS puncture
compiler. Factoring one fixed side of width `t` gives a smaller globally
generic low-core line instance with `(N',K',A')=(N-2t,K-t,A-t)` and the same
excess `h`; the other side gives a simultaneous second instance. Its affine
error rank is at most `K'+1`. This pays `t=K-1,K-2` per fixed difference.
The near-`K` subset-packing theorem now gives the missing aggregate for a
short prefix: at `t=K-c`, each residual owns disjoint `c`-subsets, so the
whole width collection has energy at most

```text
floor(C(N-2K+2c,c)/C(H+c-1,c)) M(M-1).
```

This pays `c<=2,1,1,6,5,4` on the six clean-rate rows. The unpaid task is not
another locator factorization or another fixed-codimension packing bound: it
is first-match aggregation in the deeper ranges with reduced dimensions
`K'>=3,2,2,7,6,5`, respectively. A continuation should seek a bound whose
cost does not grow exponentially in `K'`.

The pre-registered falsifier is a support-wise first-match post-strip family
at a scaled official-like row with more than `8n^3` distinct low-core slopes.
Moment growth without that slope count is not a falsifier.

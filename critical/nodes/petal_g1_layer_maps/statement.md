# Witness-exhaustive bounded sunflower atlas

- **status:** TARGET
- **consumer:** `petal_growth`

For every official row, and every rate-preserving intrinsic quotient descendant
of dyadic length at least `256`, and every received word `U` on that row, there
is an explicitly defined finite atlas `A_U` covering every top-band full-petal
contributor such that:

1. each chart is a fixed `(D0,R0)` sunflower auxiliary layer with the injective
   map `Psi` into degree-`<=d` polynomials and the exact auxiliary agreement
   `a=sigma+d+1-|R0|`; writing `ell=sigma+1` and `m` for the petal count,
   the canonical E22 remainder and P1 band pins are
   `|R0|<ell` and `d >= ell(m-2)`. The chart retains the zero constraint
   `Psi(P)|_R0=0`, as in the concrete-layer injection; this is consumed by
   K4's effective-degree reduction;
2. each chart's petals are cosets of its printed scale subgroup, so the
   periodicity scale used by G2/G3/K4 is defined;
3. the atlas has the paid weighted census

   ```text
   sum_(chart chi in A_U) (m_chi+1) <= (121/128)n^6,
   ```

   where `m_chi` is the chart's petal count; with proved K4, this bounds the
   aggregate primitive payment chart-by-chart;
4. every contributor belongs to at least one chart, so a fixed total order
   gives a first covering chart.

The weighted clause is aggregate and load-bearing. A bound on the number of
charts containing one codeword is not a substitute. The former sufficient
premise `|A_U|<=n^5` implies this weighted clause but is not required.

# Attack plan

## Construct the atlas independently of the list

The atlas index must be recoverable from bounded decomposition data attached
to `U`, not by creating one singleton chart per listed codeword. Candidate
data are a canonical core, quotient scale, retained residual profile, and the
union-injective recovery certificates already proved for distinct-label
residual-free charts.

The relevant source is the capf sunflower interface, not upstream `(A2)` by
name alone. `upstream_crosswalk.md` proves that `(A2)` pays distinct slope
projections, whereas G1 pays codeword-layer weights; no direct instance map is
known. Organize the local attack as layer catalogue, weighted payment, and
quotient closure within this one leaf.

## Required checks

1. coverage of every top-band full-petal contributor on each official row and
   every consumed rate-preserving quotient descendant of length at least 256;
2. exact transport to `def:capf-sunflower-layer`;
3. coset-petal compatibility, canonical remainder `|R0|<ell`, and P1 band
   `d>=ell(m-2)` at the scale used downstream;
4. the paid weighted census
   `sum_chi(m_chi+1)<=(121/128)n^6`, jointly over missed cores;
5. first-match coverage with no singleton-chart dependence on the listed
   codeword.

The F4 scans begin inside supplied local charts and therefore test chart
arithmetic, not arbitrary-word atlas coverage.

## Falsifiers

- an arbitrary-word contributor admitted by the top-band definition but by
  no chart;
- a family whose necessary weighted chart census exceeds `(121/128)n^6`;
- a chart whose auxiliary map or coset scale fails a downstream hypothesis;
- a quotient descendant where the atlas construction is not stable;
- any weighted census above `(121/128)n^6`.

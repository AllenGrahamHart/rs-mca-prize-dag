# Claim contract: G1 paid weighted atlas

- **claim id:** `petal_g1_layer_maps`
- **status:** TARGET, re-posed to a weaker sufficient premise
- **consumer:** `petal_growth` and `petal_small_scale_staircase_census`
- **scope:** every official row and every rate-preserving intrinsic quotient
  descendant of dyadic length `n>=256`, uniformly over received words

## Claim

G1 supplies a finite quotient-closed first-match atlas with the printed
sunflower layer dictionary and

```text
sum_(chart chi) (m_chi+1) <= (121/128)n^6.
```

Here `m_chi` is the petal count in chart `chi`.

## Sufficiency

K4 proves that the complete full-petal image in chart `chi` has size at most
`m_chi+1`. First-match assignment therefore makes the aggregate primitive
payment at most the displayed weighted census. G3 uses finite coverage and
first-match assignment, not a raw chart-count exponent. The intrinsic-scale
ledger then gives total current-plus-descendant cost below
`(64/63)(121/128)n^6=(121/126)n^6<n^6`.

## Strict weakening

The retired premise `|A_U|<=n^5` implies this claim because every chart on a
rate-`rho` row of length `n>=256` has

```text
m_chi+1 <= n-k+2 <= (121/128)n.
```

The weighted claim does not require a separate `n^5` bound on the number of
small charts. No converse is asserted.

## Nonclaims

This packet does not construct the atlas, prove catalogue completeness, or
prove uniformity over received words. It only removes a stronger-than-needed
raw chart-count premise from the open leaf.

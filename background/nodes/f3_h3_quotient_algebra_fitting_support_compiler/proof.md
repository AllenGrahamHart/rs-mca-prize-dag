# Proof

The cleared quotient polynomial is

```text
Qcal_n(T)=product_(u!=v)(c_uT-c_v),       c_u=1-zeta_n^u.
```

For each fixed `u`, its leading factor `c_u` occurs `d-1` times. Since
`product_u c_u=n`, the leading coefficient is `n^(d-1)`. This is a power of
two, so `(QAF1)` is monic over `R=Z[1/2]`, and `A_n` is free of the stated
rank.

## Finiteness

Over characteristic zero, `Qhat_n` has distinct roots: the proved quotient
consequence of shifted-product Sidonicity says every nonidentity quotient has
one ordered representation. The ordered product polynomial has root
multiplicity at most two. Therefore no root of `Qhat_n` can be a common zero
of

```text
Pcal_n^[0],...,Pcal_n^[c]
```

when `c>=2`. Their classes generate the unit ideal after tensoring `A_n` with
`Q`. Hence `M_c^X`, and its quotient `M_c^Y`, are finitely generated torsion
modules over the PID `R`. Such modules have finite odd cardinality, proving
`(QAF3)`. The inclusion `I_c^X subset I_c^Y` gives a surjection
`M_c^X -> M_c^Y`, so their orders satisfy `r_(n,c)^Y | r_(n,c)^X`.

## Reduction at an odd row prime

Tensor the presentation with `F_p`. Right exactness gives

```text
M_c^X/pM_c^X
 =F_p[T]/(Qhat_n,Pcal_n^[0],...,Pcal_n^[c]),

M_c^Y/pM_c^Y
 =F_p[T]/(Qhat_n,Pcal_n^[0],...,Pcal_n^[c],Qhat_n').  (1)
```

Reduction at `zeta_n -> g`, for a generator `g` of the order-`n` subgroup,
makes `Qhat_n` a nonzero scalar multiple of the monic quotient polynomial in
the layered-gcd compiler. At a nonidentity target `t`, its multiplicity is
`r=R(t)`, while the common gcd of the first `c+1` product derivatives has
multiplicity `q=q_c(t)`. Since `r,q<p`, differentiation gives multiplicity
`(r-1)_+` in `Qhat_n'`. A quotient of `F_p[T]` by univariate polynomials has
dimension equal to the degree of their gcd. Thus the two local contributions
to `(1)` are

```text
min(r,q),       min((r-1)_+,q),
```

which proves `(QAF4)` after summing over targets.

For a finite module over `R`, write its `p`-primary part in invariant factors
`direct_sum_i R/(p^(a_i))`. Its order has `p`-valuation `sum_i a_i`, while
the dimension after reduction modulo `p` is the number of factors. Therefore
the valuation is at least the dimension, proving `(QAF5)`.

Finally `Z_c^X>0` exactly when some target has `R(t)>0` and `q_c(t)>0`, which
is exactly `X_c>0`. Likewise `Z_c^Y>0` exactly when some target has
`R(t)>=2` and `q_c(t)>0`, which is exactly `Y_c>0`. This proves `(QAF6)`.

## Fitting presentation

In the power basis of the monic quotient algebra, multiplication by each
generator is an `d(d-1)` square matrix over `R`. Concatenating those matrices
gives a presentation of the corresponding quotient module. Since the
cokernel is finite, the matrix has full row rank over `Q`. Smith normal form
over the PID `R` says that the product of its invariant factors, equivalently
the generator of its zeroth Fitting ideal, is the module order. After
clearing only powers of two, this is the odd gcd of the full-rank minors.

## Annihilator compression

For any ideal `I` of the unital `R`-algebra `A_n`,

```text
Ann_R(A_n/I)=I intersect R.                              (6)
```

Indeed a scalar annihilates every class exactly when it annihilates the class
of one, which is exactly scalar membership in `I`. Lifting the generators of
`I` through `R[T] -> A_n` gives the displayed scalar-elimination ideals and
their Bezout certificates.

Put a presentation matrix into Smith form over the PID `R`. Finiteness gives
`D` nonzero invariant factors, with units inserted as necessary:

```text
a_1 divides a_2 divides ... divides a_D.
```

After choosing positive odd associates, the module order is `product_i a_i`
and its annihilator is generated by `a_D`. Thus

```text
a_D divides product_i a_i divides a_D^D,
```

which proves `(QAF8)`. The surjection `M_c^X -> M_c^Y` implies
`Ann(M_c^X) subset Ann(M_c^Y)`, hence `s_(n,c)^Y | s_(n,c)^X`.

Finally an odd prime divides the largest invariant factor exactly when the
module has a nonzero reduction modulo that prime. Combining this observation
with `(QAF4)` and the positivity equivalences used for `(QAF6)` proves
`(QAF9)`.
QED.

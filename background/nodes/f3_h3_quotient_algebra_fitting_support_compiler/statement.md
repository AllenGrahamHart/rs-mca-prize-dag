# H3 quotient-algebra Fitting support compiler

- **status:** PROVED
- **closure:** proof
- **consumer:** `f3_h3_mobius_excess_half` (evidence/router)
- **dependencies:** `f3_h3_global_resultant_compression`,
  `f3_h3_cutoff_layered_gcd_compiler`, `f3_h3_shifted_product_sidon`

Let `n=2^s`, `s>=2`, put `d=n-1`, and let `Pcal_n,Qcal_n in Z[T]` be the
global ordered shifted-product and cleared nonidentity quotient polynomials
from the resultant compiler. The leading coefficient of `Qcal_n` is
`n^(d-1)`. Over `R=Z[1/2]`, put

```text
Qhat_n=n^(-(d-1))Qcal_n,       A_n=R[T]/(Qhat_n). (QAF1)
```

Thus `Qhat_n` is monic and `A_n` is free of rank `d(d-1)` over `R`.

Fix `2<=c<d`. Let `I_c^X` be the ideal of `A_n` generated by the classes of
the first `c+1` Hasse derivatives of `Pcal_n`, and let

```text
I_c^Y=I_c^X+(Qhat_n'),
M_c^X=A_n/I_c^X,       M_c^Y=A_n/I_c^Y.           (QAF2)
```

Both modules are finite. Define their odd positive orders

```text
r_(n,c)^X=#M_c^X,       r_(n,c)^Y=#M_c^Y.         (QAF3)
```

For every prime `p=1 mod n`, let `P(t),R(t)` be the row product and
nonidentity quotient multiplicities and put `q_c(t)=(P(t)-c)_+`. Then

```text
dim_Fp(M_c^X/pM_c^X)=Z_c^X=sum_(t!=1) min(R(t),q_c(t)),
dim_Fp(M_c^Y/pM_c^Y)=Z_c^Y=sum_(t!=1) min((R(t)-1)_+,q_c(t)),             (QAF4)

p^(Z_c^X) divides r_(n,c)^X,
p^(Z_c^Y) divides r_(n,c)^Y,                         (QAF5)

p divides r_(n,c)^X iff X_c>0,
p divides r_(n,c)^Y iff Y_c>0.                       (QAF6)
```

Moreover `r_(n,c)^Y` divides `r_(n,c)^X`.

The full orders are unnecessary for support. Define the odd annihilator
integers

```text
(s_(n,c)^X)=I_c^X intersect R,       (s_(n,c)^Y)=I_c^Y intersect R,       (QAF7)
```

with positive odd generators. Equivalently, these are the annihilators of
`M_c^X,M_c^Y` as `R`-modules. If `D=d(d-1)`, then

```text
s_(n,c)^Y divides s_(n,c)^X,
s_(n,c)^X divides r_(n,c)^X divides (s_(n,c)^X)^D,
s_(n,c)^Y divides r_(n,c)^Y divides (s_(n,c)^Y)^D,       (QAF8)

p divides s_(n,c)^X iff X_c>0,
p divides s_(n,c)^Y iff Y_c>0.                           (QAF9)
```

The scalar elimination characterization gives exact Bezout certificates:
`s_(n,c)^X` generates

```text
(Qhat_n,Pcal_n^[0],...,Pcal_n^[c]) intersect R
```

inside `R[T]`; append `Qhat_n'` for `s_(n,c)^Y`.

The two integers are explicit Fitting integers. In the power basis of `A_n`,
concatenate the multiplication matrices of the derivative generators; for
the `Y` module append multiplication by `Qhat_n'`. The odd gcd of the
full-rank minors is the corresponding order in `(QAF3)`.

In the same Smith form, `s_(n,c)^X,s_(n,c)^Y` are just the largest invariant
factors. They are therefore the preferred exact candidate-prime outputs; the
full Fitting orders carry no additional support information.

At `c=18`, this gives an exact quotient-lift-free candidate-prime compiler
for the support of `X_18` and `Y_18`. It does not retain the full weights
`R(t)q_c(t)` or `(R(t)-1)q_c(t)`, bound either Fitting integer, or provide an
official-scale Smith/subresultant algorithm.

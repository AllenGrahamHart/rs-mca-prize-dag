# Result

Every nontrivial `u=0` loop-defect cell now has the same exact local ratio
geometry as the one-loop cell. A reused four-core loses at most `v-1` points
to denominator collapse and still exposes `h+1` points in each incident
slope fiber. The line population is therefore bounded independently of `v`
by `floor(R/(h+1))`.

The rich-core peeling formula makes this conclusion usable without assuming
regularity of the whole block family. At the three RowC rates it would close
all `u=0` cells beginning at `v=2,3,7`; the independent flat-nullity packing
already leaves only `v<=1,2,6`. Thus the theorem reaches exactly to the edge
of the current arithmetic residual and identifies the remaining need as a
cross-core ratio-fiber aggregate, not another per-line cap.

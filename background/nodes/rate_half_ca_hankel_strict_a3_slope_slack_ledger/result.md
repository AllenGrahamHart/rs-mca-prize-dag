# Result

The strict `A=3` rate-half MCA residual is now stratified for every live
parameter degree, rather than only at `e=2^37`. A failing pencil is a
degree-`e` rational normal kernel curve of exact separation rank `e+1`, with
integer slope slack `0<=h<=4(e-2^37)` and an exact near-minimum product-code
ledger. Its nonsaturated domain rows are bounded by `e+h(2^39-1)`, while at
least `T-(2^39-1-3e)` supported columns are completely domain-split.
On the sharp-cap face `h=0`, its full domain norm is a `(2^39-1)`st power of
the supported-slope polynomial up to numerator degree `O` and residual degree
at most `e`; at least `2^41-e` rows also admit the exact complementary
factorization `(SSL16)`.
Moreover each geometric component degree is forced by at most one integer
`a_i=4e_i-r_i`, and these nonnegative integers sum to `4e-(2^39-1)`.

This is a structural reduction, not a closure of the critical rate-half node.
No Modal computation was used.

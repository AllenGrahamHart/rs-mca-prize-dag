# Proof

The pair-crossing equation for `P_l` is

```text
H(b_k)D_l(b_k)^2P_l(a_k)
 +H(a_k)D_l(a_k)^2P_l(b_k)=0       (k!=l).           (1)
```

All factors occurring here are nonzero. Dividing by
`D_l(a_k)^2D_l(b_k)^2` gives

```text
r_l(a_k)/r_l(b_k)=-H(a_k)/H(b_k),                   (2)
```

which is independent of `l`. Hence, for distinct `l,m` and
`k notin {l,m}`, the rational function

```text
f_lm=r_l/r_m=P_lD_m^2/(P_mD_l^2)                    (3)
```

satisfies

```text
f_lm(a_k)=f_lm(b_k).                                 (4)
```

Every nonconstant `f_lm` has degree at most eight.

Suppose first that one `f_lm` is constant. Polynomial equality in `(3)`
and coprimality of `D_l,D_m` imply `D_l^2|P_l` and `D_m^2|P_m`.
The degree bounds force

```text
P_l=c_lD_l^2,       P_m=c_mD_m^2.                   (5)
```

Substituting the first identity into `(1)` gives `(QFR3)` for every
`k!=l`; the second supplies the missing pair `k=l`. This proves the first
alternative. We henceforth assume that every `f_lm` is nonconstant.

Fix index `1` and form the intermediate function field

```text
K=F_p(f_1m:2<=m<=e) subset F_p(X),
d=[F_p(X):K].                                        (6)
```

We use twice the following generator-compression observation. If a subfield
of `F_p(X)` is generated by rational functions of degree at most eight,
one may select generators successively. After the first selection the
extension index is at most eight; every strict enlargement of the selected
subfield divides that index by an integer at least two. Consequently four
selected functions suffice when the final index is one, and three suffice
when the final index is at least two.

We claim that `d>1`. Otherwise choose at most four of the `f_1m` which
generate `F_p(X)`. For every pair whose index is neither `1` nor one of
those selected indices, `(4)` gives one common off-diagonal collision of
all selected functions, in both orientations.

For a degree-at-most-eight rational function, its projective coincidence
divisor after removing the diagonal has bidegree at most `(7,7)`. Since the
selected functions generate `F_p(X)`, their off-diagonal coincidence
divisors have no common irreducible component. Choose one nonempty divisor.
It has at most seven geometric components. Because `p>7`, a base-field
linear combination of the other coincidence equations avoids every one of
those components. Bezout on `P^1 x P^1` then bounds the common ordered
off-diagonal collisions by

```text
7*7+7*7=98.                                          (7)
```

If the first divisor is empty, its function already has degree one and
there are no off-diagonal collisions. In either case the official packet,
which supplies at least `2(e-5)>98` distinct ordered collisions, is
impossible. Thus `d>=2`.

Luroth's theorem now gives

```text
K=F_p(psi),       deg psi=d.                         (8)
```

Because `K` contains a nonconstant function of degree at most eight,
`2<=d<=8`. The official characteristic exceeds eight, so `psi` is
separable. Select at most three functions `f_1j=g_j(psi)` which generate
`K`. Their outer functions `g_j` generate `F_p(T)` and have degree at most
`floor(8/d)<=4`.

The same coincidence-divisor argument on the `T`-line shows that the tuple
`(g_j)` identifies at most

```text
2*3*3=18                                             (9)
```

ordered pairs of distinct points of `P^1`. Each such ordered pair has at
most `d^2` ordered preimages under `psi`. Among the at least `e-4`
exceptional pairs not indexed by `1` or a selected generator, at most
`9d^2` therefore have distinct `psi`-values. Hence at least

```text
e-4-9d^2                                             (10)
```

pairs lie in fibers of `psi`.

It remains to exclude `d>=5`. In that range every nonconstant
`f_1m=g_m(psi)` has `deg g_m=1`, because its degree is at most eight.
Thus `g_m` is Mobius and `deg f_1m=d`. At a root `x` of `D_m`, with
`m!=1`, the nonvanishing assertion for `P_1` gives

```text
ord_x(f_1m)=2-ord_x(P_m).                            (11)
```

If `D_m` does not divide `P_m`, at least one of its two roots has right-hand
side two. Since `g_m` is Mobius, that root is ramified for `psi`. Distinct
bad indices use distinct roots. Riemann--Hurwitz for the tame degree-`d`
map `P^1 -> P^1` therefore leaves at most `2d-2` bad indices.

There are consequently two distinct indices `m,n!=1` for which

```text
P_m=D_mL_m,       P_n=D_nL_n,       deg L_m,deg L_n<=2.   (12)
```

For them

```text
f_mn=r_m/r_n=L_mD_n/(L_nD_m)                        (13)
```

has degree at most four. It is nonconstant, since a constant `f_mn` would
give the already separated global antiweight alternative. But `f_mn` lies
in `F_p(psi)`, so its degree is a positive multiple of `d>=5`, contradicting
`(13)`. Therefore `d in {2,3,4}`. Substitution in `(10)` gives the stated
uniform lower bound `e-148`. QED.

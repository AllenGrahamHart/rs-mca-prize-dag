The relative antipodal weight `53/42` in `(A6S3)` is deliberate.
Antipodal-free fibers obey the sharper distance-four cap `N_4<=m`, while the
antipodal lane keeps the uniform cap but gains the norm-one centroid saving
and its additional subgroup-membership test.

The incidence equality counts every distance-six edge in an antipodal target,
not only edges incident to the antipodal representation. The canonical
antipodal root fixes the target; two additional roots independently recover
the two edge endpoints.

distance-six edge–quotient records, and the direct and routed counts agree.
This checks posedness only; no target there reaches `P>=33`.

The `E=6,10,14` interfaces are Pareto alternatives, not nested proofs. Lower
cutoffs enlarge the target set while increasing the budget. Candidate-prime
generation still prefers `E=14` because its weighted center degree is twelve;
an analytic incidence proof may instead benefit from the `E=6` allowance.

# Audit

The theorem is scoped to the seven-vector selection, not every unordered root
pair. Diagonal pairs and pairs containing `-1` were removed before the norm
one/norm three dichotomy is used.

The cross-overlap is an equality of signed monomial atoms after folding by
`zeta^(n/2)=-1`. A positive atom matching a negative atom therefore means
`uv=-y`, not `uv=y`.

The same-kind exclusion uses equality of the two shifted products in the
finite row. It is false as a statement about two arbitrary characteristic-zero
coefficient vectors of distance four.

Equation `(D4R3)` reduces the generic lane to two free subgroup variables but
does not prove that the resulting value lies outside the subgroup. It is a
router, not a closure theorem.

Likewise, `(D4A)` requires square-subgroup membership of `u+v-uv`. It does not
assert that every pair `(u,v)` gives an antipodal collision.

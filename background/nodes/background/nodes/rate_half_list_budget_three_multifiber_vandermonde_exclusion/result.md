# Result

The earlier single-fiber quotient exclusion now extends to arbitrary unions
of equal fibers for the six cycle/path chambers. The top coefficient block of
each one-root-deleted locator remembers its deleted root as a Vandermonde
column, so the constant split-unit relation cannot occur when the quotient
fiber has size at least four.

At the prize-max power-of-two row, direct quotient-periodic cycle/path
constructions are reduced to fiber sizes one and two. Primitive, mixed-map,
and partial-fiber couplings remain open, as do the other seven chambers.

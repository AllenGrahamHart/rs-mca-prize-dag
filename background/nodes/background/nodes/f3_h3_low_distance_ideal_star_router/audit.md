common-product fiber. Positivity alone forces four low edges; 2-primary
exclusion raises this to six. Characteristic-zero shifted-product Sidonicity
still supplies distinctness of the seven coefficient vectors.

The factor four in `(ISR4)` is also structural. Every shifted product contains
two factors divisible by `pi=1-zeta`, and `N((pi)^2)=4`. The candidate object
is the normalized ideal after removing this universal two-adic factor; an
unnormalized gcd would retain a known irrelevant factor.

The rooted center is not freely permutable with a leaf unless the resulting
two distances also satisfy `(ISR2)`. The stated orbit reduction uses only odd
Galois dilation, internal pair exchanges, and exchange of `F,G`.

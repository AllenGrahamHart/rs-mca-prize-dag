# Audit

The ODE is asserted only for a complete canonical survivor. Primary parity by
itself does not supply the split outer quartic or the exact degree-one
residual.

The parity changes under reversal because `deg U` is odd and `deg V` is even.
Thus even reverse polynomials produce `U=YU_0(Y^2)` and `V=V_0(Y^2)`, not two
even original-coordinate polynomials.

The forced zero of `U` at the origin is not an excluded degeneration. It is
simple and consumes exactly the sole exceptional root permitted by the
linear residual. Any symbolic saturation that removes this branch would
remove the entire deleted-pair generic sublocus.

The recurrence is a compression, not an exclusion. Its terminal equation is
necessary for polynomial termination, while the separate square-pencil and
Möbius conditions remain live.

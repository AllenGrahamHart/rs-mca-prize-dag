# Audit

The fixed-root simplification applies to the union over all admissible stars,
not to one fixed labeled star. For a fixed star, `(PAC1)` retains the odd
conjugate `g^r`; moving that `r` into the exponents changes the labeled star.

The assumption `p=1 mod n` is load-bearing. It makes every prime above `p`
degree one and identifies its residue evaluation with a primitive `n`th root
in `F_p`. The criterion is not asserted for inert or partially split primes.

Removing the universal `pi^2` factor is harmless only because `p` is odd and
`g^r!=1`, so `pi` is a unit at every selected prime above `p`.

# Attack surface

Writing all elements as shifts of subgroup roots, `(FM69)` counts

```text
(1-x)(1-y)=(1-u)(1-v)=(1-w)/(1-z),
```

where the two ordered product pairs are distinct. This is a codimension-two
six-variable subgroup incidence system. The constant `69` has a factor of
more than `34` over the measured boundary ratio `1.9716`.

Useful routes:

1. apply a multivariate Stepanov polynomial directly to the two-equation
   system, separating repeated/swap components first;
2. use the proved quotient-block identity and control the product factorial
   moment within each coefficient block;
3. derive a constants-explicit oriented character estimate.

A marginal `O(n^2 log n)` shifted-energy bound does not imply `(FM69)` with
the required printed constant.

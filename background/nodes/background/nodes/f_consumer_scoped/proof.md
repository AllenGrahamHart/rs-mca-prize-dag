# f_consumer_scoped refutation

The scoped claim required every F-consuming path in the proof to pass through
one of two classical families:

1. coordinate-slice/fiber flats, whose direction spaces are shortened
   Reed-Solomon codes and hence MDS;
2. Hankel-pencil kernel flats, whose sparse words are governed by the
   displacement-rank identities.

The A0 audit found a third consumer: the L1 ImgFib mixed-petal sunflower
residual. That residual is not a coordinate-prefix/fiber flat and is not a
Hankel-pencil kernel flat. Therefore the two-family audit does not cover all F
consumers.

The two scoped branches remain valid local tools, but the global conclusion
that general F can be demoted is false.

extension-code list problem with exact parameters, rather than only as a
two-variable basis-deficiency table.  The replay reproduces the P-A/P-B
residual counts

An independent finite fixture over `F_101` constructs a full-affine-rank-five
list in a one-dimensional extension of `GRS_4`.  Its codewords occupy distinct
quotient classes, agree with one received word on exactly five coordinates,
and have pairwise agreement at most four.  This validates the reduction's
object and also shows why ordinary subset packing remains a genuine barrier.

The theorem does not bound the extension list and does not promote either XR
consumer.  The next useful input must exploit the one-dimensional extension
and the retained polynomial subcode, especially at `u=v=0`; another count of
bases inside the same four-dimensional subcode cannot improve that base case.

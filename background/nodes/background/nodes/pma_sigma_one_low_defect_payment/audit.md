# Audit - PMA sigma-one low-defect payment

1. The theorem sums over exact `R_P`, not arbitrary chosen background zeros.
   Since the maximal background has one point, there is one cell at each of
   `r=0,1`; no factor two is missing.
2. The denominator is `binom(kappa+sigma,kappa)=kappa+1`. It is the minimum
   interpolation-owner charge, not an assumed exact agreement size.
3. The factor `binom(k-1,d)` restores every missed-core set. The theorem is
   therefore stronger than a per-`D` statement but remains per sunflower.
4. Periodic members are harmless overcount. The theorem is applied after the
   global owner only by taking a subset of the class counted here.
5. No `q` factor is present: interpolation bases, rather than arbitrary
   cofactor coefficients, own the source list.
6. The `n^5/1024` line does not itself prove a global `n^6` payment. A separate
   first-match chart census of at most `1024n` weighted units, or a sharper
   direct global owner, would be needed.

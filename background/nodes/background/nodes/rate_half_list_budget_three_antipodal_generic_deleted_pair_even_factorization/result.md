# Result

The complete deleted-pair survivor is now an exact partition of a punctured
torsion binomial into two equal-degree square-pencil factors. This is a more
structured split-pencil census target than a large resultant or fixed-order
point sweep.

Equivalently, the two inverse-root cells have identical Fourier moments for
the first `2M` frequencies, exactly one quarter of the ambient order. The
first unequal moment is `-h(lambda-mu)` at frequency `h=2M+1`. This is the
natural interface to quotient/prefix-flatness arguments.

The node does not close the sublocus. The remaining task is to show that no
primitive nonperiodic partition `(DEF3)` can satisfy the Möbius matching, or
to print and route any exceptional partition.

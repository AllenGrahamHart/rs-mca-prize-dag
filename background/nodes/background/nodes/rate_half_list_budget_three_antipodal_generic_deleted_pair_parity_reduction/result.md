# Result

The deleted-antipodal-pair sublocus no longer has a four-root or nested-series
search. It is one torsion ratio `t`, one primary polynomial `F_(2M)(t)`, and
one remaining secondary coefficient `G_M(t)`.

The next mathematical target is a characteristic-explicit coprimality or
torsion nonvanishing theorem for these two functions. Finite scans of more
orders do not close that target and are not requested.

For `M<=5`, exact elimination finds that every prime capable of supporting
the primary, secondary, and dyadic torsion equations simultaneously is at
most `4M-1`. The preferred proof target is a uniform good-characteristic
version of that observation; fixed-`M` extension is not a substitute.

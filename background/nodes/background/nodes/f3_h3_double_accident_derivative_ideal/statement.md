Throughout this theorem, `n=2^s` is one of the 29 official orders
`13<=s<=41`. The order-eight computation below is only a generalized
cutoff-two fixture.

Use the dyadic cyclotomic notation of
`f3_h3_global_derivative_ideal_valuation`. Thus

```text
K=Q(zeta_n),       O=Z[zeta_n],       d=[K:Q]=n/2,
I={(u,v):1<=u,v<n, u!=v},
gamma_uv=(1-zeta^v)/(1-zeta^u),
B_n=min(n-19,ceil(33n^(2/3))-19),
```

and `J_uv` is the integral ideal generated by the first nineteen coefficients
of the cleared shifted-product polynomial at `gamma_uv`.

For `i=(u,v)` and `j=(u',v')` in `I`, put

```text
eta_ij=(1-zeta^v)(1-zeta^u')-(1-zeta^v')(1-zeta^u),
Delta_i=product_(j!=i) eta_ij,
K_i=J_i+(Delta_i)^B_n O.                                (JD1)
```

All `Delta_i` are nonzero. Define

```text
K_n=product_(i in I) K_i,
F_n=odd part of Norm_O(K_n).
```

There is a unique positive odd integer `f_n` such that

```text
F_n=f_n^d.                                               (JD2)
```

If `e_n` is the earlier global derivative-ideal integer, then

```text
f_n divides e_n.                                         (JD3)
```

There is also an exact, but generally weaker, global-discriminant envelope.
Put `M=(n-1)(n-2)` and

```text
Q_n(T)=product_(i=(u,v) in I) ((1-zeta^u)T-(1-zeta^v)) in Z[T],
h_n=odd part of |Disc(Q_n)|.
```

Then

```text
f_n divides gcd(e_n,h_n^B_n).                            (JD3a)
```

Now let `p=1 mod n` be an official row prime and define

```text
Y_18=sum_(t!=1)(P(t)-18)_+ max(R(t)-1,0).
```

Then

```text
p^Y_18 divides f_n,                                      (JD4)
p divides f_n  iff  Y_18>0.                              (JD5)
```

Consequently every official prime outside the strictly refined exceptional
set of `f_n` has `Y_18=0` and therefore satisfies C36'. More generally,

```text
17 log(f_n) <= (283n^2+34n-17) log(p)                    (JD6)
```

implies the exact residual bound and closes the row.

The first term in `B_n` uses the exact fiber cap `P(t)<=|A|=n-1`; the second
uses the uniform product-fiber theorem. Thus the separator exponent is at
most linear and is of order `n^(2/3)` once the Stepanov term is smaller. This
theorem still does not bound or factor `f_n`.
It proves that the exceptional

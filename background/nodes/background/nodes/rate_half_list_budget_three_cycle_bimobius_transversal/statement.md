# Budget-three 4-cycle bi-Mobius transversal

- **status:** PROVED
- **closure:** proof
- **consumer:** `rate_half_list_adjacent_crossing`

Take the canonical 4-cycle row of the budget-three split-pencil normal form.
Write

```text
E_02={r_02},   E_03={r_03},
E_12={r_12},   E_13={r_13},
```

where the four points are distinct members of `D`. There is no singleton or
full block, and

```text
deg A_0=deg A_1=deg A_2=deg A_3=d-1.
```

The four tight edge quotients are constants, so

```text
b_ij=q_ij(X-r_ij),       ij in {02,03,12,13}.
```

Put `theta=A_1/A_0`. Its four large block fibers are

```text
theta=infinity                         on T_0,
theta=0                                on T_1,
theta=M_2(X)=b_12/b_02                 on T_2,
theta=M_3(X)=b_13/b_03                 on T_3.       (CBM1)
```

Both `M_2` and `M_3` are nonconstant injective Mobius maps. The bounded edge
gate also gives the exact graph-difference identity

```text
M_3-M_2 = b_01 b_23/(b_02 b_03).                    (CBM2)
```

Every projective member of the constant pencil generated by `A_0,A_1`, other
than those two block locators, has at most six distinct roots in `D`: at most
one from each of `T_2,T_3` and at most the four edge points. Consequently,
for `d>=8`, this degree-`d-1` pencil has exactly two fully `D`-split members,
namely `A_0` and `A_1`.

Thus the 4-cycle is not an unstructured exception to the split-fiber atlas.
At official scale it is a primitive rational map with two complete constant
fibers and two injective Mobius graph fibers. This theorem does not exclude
that object or determine the budget-three crossing.

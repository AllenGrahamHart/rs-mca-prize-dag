# Audit

- The common root used against `(TFE1)` exists over a splitting field because
  the proved gcd degree is positive; no base-field rational root is assumed.
- `P_cl(gamma_0)` is nonzero by the definition of the exceptional slope.
- Divisibility `Q_gamma|P_X` is obtained for every supported slope only after
  the exceptional allocation is fixed, so that `P_clZ_B=P`.
- A root of `B_X` cannot also be a root of `P_X`; both are complementary
  factors of squarefree `G_X`.
- The capacity equality `C_*=ce_*` counts every bad row once with its full
  deficit and uses no average.
- Equality in `C_*<=e_*` is checked exactly: `e_*-C_*=4b+1-D_*`.
- The final contradiction uses the exceptional degree `r-1`, not the generic
  degree `r`. Replacing it by `r` would erase the one-degree gap.
- Specialization cannot have larger `X`-degree than the global polynomial
  `A_1`.
- The active-trace branch remains open.

# Result

The remaining deleted-pair generic branch has no free polynomial direction.
For nonharmonic `q`, one Euclidean quotient is the only possible scaled
square `V_0^2`, and one remainder identity checks it. For harmonic `q`, the
single residual `Q-A^2` must be the negative of a fourth power.

The official exclusion problem is therefore a torsion-indexed deterministic
square test in the lift ratio `r` and at most three ratios `q`, rather than a
component search over official-degree `U,V` coefficients.

The test is not yet proved to reject uniformly. A contributor computation is
valuable only if it uses a compressed representation with complete coverage
of the official torsion family.

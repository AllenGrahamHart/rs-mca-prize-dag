# Audit

- Parameter degree alone does not imply independent coefficient vectors.
  Independence comes from strict equivalence to the unique right singular
  block `L_m`; the proof does not make the invalid inference from degree.
- Strict equivalence uses a constant invertible column transformation, so it
  preserves the independence of the canonical monomial coefficient vectors.
- The right nullity is exactly one.  This is what makes the primitive kernel
  generator unique up to scalar and prevents mixing among several right
  singular blocks.
- Separation rank here is the two-factor tensor rank, equivalently matrix
  flattening rank.  It is not a higher-order tensor-rank assertion.
- The rational-normal statement holds over the base field and in arbitrary
  characteristic because it uses the monomial basis of `O(m)`.
- Evaluation is injective only because `rho<N`; no probabilistic or generic
  evaluation claim is used.
- The hyperplane-section counts are imported from the proved exact endpoint
  ledger.  Rational-normal geometry alone does not imply or contradict them.
- The verifier checks dimensions, canonical coefficient independence at
  small indices, official arithmetic, and DAG wiring.  It is not a computer
  proof of Kronecker canonical form.

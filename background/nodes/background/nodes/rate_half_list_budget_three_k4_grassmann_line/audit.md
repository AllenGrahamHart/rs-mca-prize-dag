# Audit

- Odd characteristic is explicit and includes every official prime-field
  row. It is used when polarizing `B wedge B=0`.
- The explicit `d>=3` scope makes every degree-`d-2` block nonempty, as used
  in the no-proper-subsum argument.
- Distinct edge roots prove both that `B_0,B_1` are independent and that no
  annihilator coordinate can vanish.
- The locator vector enters the moving two-plane through the already-proved
  polynomial Plucker parameterization; no division at an edge root is used in
  the theorem.
- The coefficients in `(KGL4)` lie in the base field. They come from the
  fixed base-field three-space `<w,u,v>`, not from `F(X)`.
- Nondegeneracy uses monicity and disjoint nonempty `T_i`; it is stronger than
  merely having one four-term dependence.
- The exceptional degree is exact: two full coordinates plus six edge
  coordinates. There are no singleton coordinates in the `K_4` row.
- The theorem is a reduction to a split-unit equation, not an application of
  an unproved S-unit estimate.

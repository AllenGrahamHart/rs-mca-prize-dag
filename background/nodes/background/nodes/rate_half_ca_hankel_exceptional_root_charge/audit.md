# Audit

- The proof charges rank loss with multiplicity, not merely the number of
  exceptional slopes.  A rank loss `c` costs at least `c` in the local Smith
  form of the regular determinant.
- At an exceptional supported slope, the specialized minimal apolar
  generator divides both `Q_gamma` and the split locator.  This is why the
  latter forces retained domain roots of `Q_gamma`; merely knowing that the
  enlarged kernel contains a split locator would not suffice.
- Subtracting the `s` fixed roots is deliberately pessimistic: it allows all
  fixed roots to occur in the specialized minimal generator.
- The official `A=3` corollary does not use the unaudited Laurent/Ritt or
  pullback-classification nodes.  Those routes remain irrelevant unless the
  final core-free high-degree profile is attacked structurally.
- The `A=1` range list is a necessary profile sieve, not an existence claim.
  The residual determinant charge closes the formerly retained
  `s=2,e=2^37` corner; every surviving row has `e>=2^37+1`.
- In the contracted charge, `j` counts fixed factors omitted by the special
  minimal generator.  Those omissions are not discarded: each increases the
  roots retained in `Qbar` by one and decreases the residual rank-loss charge
  by one, preserving an exact total of `d`.
- A nonzero regular Kronecker size does not imply a fixed factor of the
  generic apolar generator.  The exact `F_101`, `r=4`, `e=1`, `delta=1`
  fixture has coprime endpoint generators and is retained as a mutation
  control against that shortcut.
- The verifier checks the coupled integer inequality, the endpoint split,
  and the DAG wiring.  It is an arithmetic guard, not a substitute for the
  apolar and Smith-form proof.

# Audit

- The result is restricted to the strict budget, where `r=rho` and the
  generic right nullity is one. No claim is silently transferred to the
  half-distance pencil, whose right nullity is two.
- Independence of `Q_0,...,Q_e` comes from the unique `L_e` Kronecker block
  under constant strict equivalence. It is not inferred from generic
  coefficient behavior.
- The core-free conclusion `s=0` is required to make every domain row
  nonzero. It is supplied by the proved exceptional-root theorem throughout
  the stated live range.
- `d_x` counts distinct finite parameter roots. A boundary root consumes
  degree but does not count as a supported finite slope; it therefore only
  increases the capacity deficit and cannot invalidate the inequalities.
- The exact matrix rank `e+1` is asserted only under failure, where
  `T>=rho+2>e`; this guarantees injective evaluation in the parameter
  variable.
- The old endpoint is recovered without changing constants:
  `e=m` forces `h=0`, `T=4m+1`, and `C=1+O<=m`.
- The norm and complementary-factor identities are asserted only on `h=0`.
  Their interpolation uses at least `N-e>0` saturated rows; no empty-set
  interpolation is hidden.
- `Vbar` is an interpolated complementary factor, not a second apolar
  generator. The proof never gives it Hankel-kernel status.
- The component interval is a necessary arithmetic chamber, not an existence
  theorem. A parameter degree for which the interval contains no integer is
  excluded for that component; a contained integer does not construct one.
- Positivity of component bidegrees uses primitivity, core-freeness, and the
  existence of clean split specializations. It is not assumed from generic
  irreducibility.
- No computation beyond tiny integer identities is used as proof evidence.

# Audit

- All gcds are fiberwise over a splitting field. No claim that they glue to a
  global polynomial is made.
- At a root of `B_X`, `P_X(x)` is nonzero because the two products partition
  the distinct factors of `G_X`. This scalar is cancelled only there.
- `epsilon_x` records only the one exceptional supported slope. Thus
  `v_x=a_x+epsilon_x` and `deg Qhat_x=delta_x+epsilon_x` are exact.
- The exceptional-slope specialization need not retain full `X`-degree.
  Accordingly only `deg Qhat_0<=c+1`, not equality, is claimed.
- The lower bound `a_0>=r-1-c` counts distinct domain roots and uses the
  proved exceptional deficit `D_*=1`.
- Vanishing of a trace means identically as a polynomial in the remaining
  variable. Pointwise vanishing at a sample is insufficient for descent.
- Factor allocation is performed prime by prime. If a factor divides both
  `W` and `B`, either allocation is valid; no uniqueness is claimed.
- `K_1` is nonzero because the dependency proves `K!=0`.
- Neither the active-trace nor unit-product branch is asserted impossible.

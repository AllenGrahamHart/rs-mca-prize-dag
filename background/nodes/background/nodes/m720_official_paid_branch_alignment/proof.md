# proof: m720_official_paid_branch_alignment

An M720 active-core candidate in the `h=7..20` band is an anchored split
`h`-trade candidate: two disjoint `h`-subsets with matching elementary
signatures through degree `h-1`, with the toral/full-fiber cases treated as
paid by the existing ledger.

The proved node `a_universal_trade_variety` puts these row trades on the
row-independent x83 square-shift obstruction variety `W_h`. The proved node
`x83_uniform_square_shift_obstruction_gate` gives the complete dichotomy for
such finite-row minimal trades: the trade is either a characteristic-zero
full-fiber class, which is paid, or it is a `p`-specific event where the row
prime divides one of the cleared low-obstruction cyclotomic norms.

The M720 MITM gate is supporting evidence that the local scanner uses the same
paid/toral versus non-toral split, but the proof here is the structural x83
dichotomy. Therefore every official-shape `h=7..20` active-core candidate is
accounted for by the paid branch or by the norm-gate branch. This node does
not assert that the norm-gate branch is empty at the official rows.

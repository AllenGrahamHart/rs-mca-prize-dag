# Claim contract

- **Claim:** quotient separation unit-normalizes the global product-rich
  derivative ideal on every `R=1` fiber, producing an odd integer `f_n|e_n`
  with `p^Y_18|f_n` and exact support `p|f_n iff Y_18>0` on official rows.
- **Quantifiers:** every dyadic order `n>=4`; every odd official row prime
  `p=1 mod n` for the local conclusions.
- **Dependencies:** `f3_h3_global_derivative_ideal_valuation` for `J_i` and
  its Hasse valuation; `f3_h3_cutoff18_double_accident_reduction` for the
  residual consumer; `f3_h3_uniform_product_fiber_stepanov` for the optimized
  exponent `B_n`; dyadic nonidentity quotient uniqueness transitively.
- **Consumer:** exceptional-prime route for
  `f3_h3_mobius_excess_half`.
- **New remaining input:** bound or factor the strictly smaller integer `f_n`,
  or prove `(JD6)` at its official prime divisors.
- **Route fence:** the global quotient discriminant gives the valid envelope
  `f_n|gcd(e_n,h_n^B_n)`, but its naive height loses more than seven orders of
  magnitude and its order-eight fixture does not improve `e_8` at all.
- **Nonclaims:** no size bound, factorization, efficient official-order
  construction, valuation equality, or independence of the two accidents.
- **Falsifier:** a zero `Delta_i` in characteristic zero, an official product
  excess above `B_n=min(n-19,ceil(33n^(2/3))-19)`, loss of Galois
  invariance, an official prime with `Y_18>0` not dividing `f_n`, or an
  official prime dividing `f_n` with `Y_18=0`.
- **Replay:** `tools/ramguard tiny -- python3
  background/nodes/f3_h3_double_accident_derivative_ideal/verify.py`.

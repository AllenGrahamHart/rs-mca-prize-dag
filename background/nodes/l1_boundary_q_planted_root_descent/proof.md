# Proof - L1 boundary-Q planted-root descent

## 1. Determinant identity

Represent an affine exact member before monic normalization by

```text
v=(W,WP)=A g_1+B g_2,       B!=0.
```

Its first coordinate is a nonzero scalar multiple of the monic complement
locator `Omega/L`.  Taking the determinant with `g_1` gives

```text
W(W_1P-N_1)=det(g_1,v)=B gamma Omega.
```

Canceling the complement locator and absorbing its leading scalar into the
nonzero constant gives `(PR1)`.  The constant may depend on the projective
normalization of the member; no fixed affine normalization is assumed.

## 2. Canonical planted roots

At a root `s` of `D`, the module congruence gives

```text
N_1(s)=W_1(s)U(s)=0.
```

The domain polynomial is squarefree, so `D|N_1`.  Evaluating `(PR1)` at `s`
then gives `L(s)=0`; hence `S` is contained in every agreement set in this
one boundary cell.  Because the member is exact, its codeword also satisfies
`P(s)=U(s)`.

Divide `(PR1)` by `D` to obtain `(PR3)`.  By the definition of `S`, `W_1`
has no root in `H'`; consequently `gcd(Wbar_1,Omega')=1`.  For every `x` in
`H'`, division of the module congruence by `D(x)` gives

```text
Nbar_1(x)=Wbar_1(x)U(x).
```

Since `Wbar_1(x)` is nonzero, `(PR3)` vanishes at `x` exactly when
`P(x)=U(x)`.  Therefore a split degree-`m-r` divisor on the right gives all
and only the agreements outside `S`.  Together with the planted conditions
on `S`, this proves both directions of the asserted bijection and excludes
extra agreements.

## 3. Dimension and preserved depth

Evaluation of degree-below-`k` polynomials at `r` distinct points has rank
`min(k,r)`.  Thus for `r>=k` there is at most one candidate.  For `r<k`,
the interpolation polynomial `P_S` exists uniquely with degree below `r`.
The vanishing ideal of `S` is generated by `D`, so the complete set of
degree-below-`k` solutions is exactly `(PR4)`, uniquely, with
`deg R<k-r`.

Substitution into `(PR3)` gives `(PR5).`  The locator degree and candidate
dimension both fall by `r`, proving `(PR6)`.  Since `D` is determined by the
fixed basis vector `g_1`, this is one canonical descent, not a sum over root
subsets.

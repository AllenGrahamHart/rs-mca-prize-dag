# Result

Every first-checkpoint minimum-width collision is a low-degree Frobenius
split pencil: two fully domain-split fibers of `Z^p+Q`, with
`deg Q<=2p-d-1`. At terminal depth the perturbation is linear, its fibers are
affine `F_p`-lines, and multiplicative ratio-set growth excludes them from an
official smooth coset. More generally, the nonzero fiber has ratio
multiplicity at most `2p-d-1`; this excludes `t=p` throughout
`d>=2p-1-r_*(p,n)`, where
`r_*(p,n)=floor((p(p-1)-1)/(n-1))`. Uniformly
`r_*(p,n)>=floor(11(p-1)/256)`. The surviving lower band is the higher-degree
split-pencil census.

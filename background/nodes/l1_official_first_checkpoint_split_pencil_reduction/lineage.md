# Lineage

The tame-tail theorem raised the first checkpoint collision width to `p` but
left that stratum untyped. At degree `p`, the Wronskian upper bound forces the
two monic tail locators to differ by a constant. This identifies the whole
stratum with a low-degree Frobenius split pencil and lets multiplicative
ratio growth close its terminal affine-line endpoint.

Applying ratio growth to the nonzero one of the two fibers extends that
closure from the terminal line to every perturbation degree at most
the row-sharp ratio threshold `floor((p(p-1)-1)/(n-1))`.

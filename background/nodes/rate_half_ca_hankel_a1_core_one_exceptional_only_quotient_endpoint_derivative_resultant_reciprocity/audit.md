# Audit

1. The resultant convention is monic-first, so no leading coefficient of
   `A` appears in `(2)`.
2. Simple vanishing at zero gives `q_1(a)!=0`; exact degree gives
   `q_e(a)!=0`.
3. The exponent `2e` is the least possible degree in the product of the
   `2e` row polynomials, so its coefficient is exactly the product of the
   `q_1(a)`.
4. `P_ord` is monic, so the profile scalar is exactly `Res(A,q_e)` rather
   than merely proportional to it.
5. In the swapped identity, both minus signs cancel. In square classes,
   `[z_max^(-1)]=[z_max]`.

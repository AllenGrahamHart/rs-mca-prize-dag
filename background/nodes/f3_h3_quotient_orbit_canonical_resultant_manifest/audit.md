# Audit

1. The common power of two is removed before normalization; otherwise the
   odd-unit action cannot send a general coordinate to one.
2. The odd/odd family excludes `w=1`, and both even families exclude zero.
   These are exactly the diagonal and identity-coordinate exclusions.
3. The reverse family is needed only when the first normalized coordinate is
   even. Odd/odd reversal already appears as another member of the `+`
   family.
4. For even `w`, the reciprocal resultant has leading coefficient
   `2^(2^v_2(w)-1)`. It is monic only after dividing by that unit in
   `Z[1/2]`.
5. Reciprocal orbit polynomials do not imply equal cutoff-35 scalar support:
   product richness is not asserted to be invariant under `t -> 1/t`.
6. The formulas remove orbit enumeration, not coefficient growth, total
   degree, scalar elimination, factorization, or the survivor-moment problem.

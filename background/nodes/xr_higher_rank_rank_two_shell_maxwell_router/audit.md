# Audit

- The zero sets lie in the active union, so no coordinate is zero in every
  row. This makes them disjoint by the rank-two projective-kernel argument.
- Pairwise support intersection is bounded by selected-block intersection;
  equality between a support and its block is never assumed.
- The union estimate charges every coordinate saved by block extension to a
  new block-pair incidence. The inequality `C(r,2)>=r-1` is load-bearing.
- The worst-case substitution is `Z<=min(td,a+d+1)`. Using only `Z<=td`
  loses the special RowC `h=3,a=4` payment.
- A positive deficit excludes only a relation supported on every block of
  its minimal Maxwell core. Proper local rank-two circuits remain real.
- The top-shell parity statement uses `t>=4`; it is not a claim about an
  arbitrary two-dimensional polynomial pencil with fewer selected rows.

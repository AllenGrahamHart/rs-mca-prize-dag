# Audit

The second quotient representation is used to select a nonzero coupling
anchor, not discarded. For a fixed product lift, characteristic-zero quotient
uniqueness permits at most one zero coupling. Thus `R(t)>=2` is exactly what
guarantees at least one nonzero generator, and more generally supplies at
least `R(t)-1` of them.

The coarse three-generator candidate ideal is an outer gate. It deliberately
does not encode the second quotient collision. That collision is restored by
the exact finite-row `R(t)` screen, while omitting it from symbolic template
generation removes one ordered quotient pair from the candidate coordinates.

No lower bound for an ideal index follows merely from having `R(t)-1`
generators. The batching theorem records common-prime alignment and a stronger
sieve; it is not an independence claim.

The exact zero-locus classification is stronger than the nonzero-selection
argument needs, but makes exclusion of zero templates an elementary exponent
test. It is the previously banked characteristic-zero three-to-one
classification, now connected to the live C36 route.

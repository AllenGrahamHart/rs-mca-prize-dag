# Audit

The degree-three claim concerns generic neighbors only. A generic vertex may
also meet the unique antipodal representation, giving total degree four.

The proof counts unordered neighbor pairs. The two choices `uv=-x,-y` and the
reverse-overlap root `-xy` are certificates; coincidences between them only
decrease the number of distinct neighbors.

The pseudoforest count chooses exactly one valid orientation per edge. For a
fixed head pair, its product fixes one root of the tail and the common shifted
product fixes the other. This is an indegree bound; summing the local
degree-three estimate would lose the new factor.

The bound is scoped to one common finite-field product fiber. The cross-
overlap theorem cannot be applied to arbitrary characteristic-zero vectors of
distance four.

`(D4C2)` is exact as a uniform envelope for the possible antipodal branch. In
an antipodal-free fiber, `(D4C1)` gives the sharper `N_4(t)<=m`.

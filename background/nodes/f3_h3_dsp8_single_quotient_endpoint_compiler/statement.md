Every positive summand has a pure disjoint-distance-six product star of
degree at least eight in class `0` or six in class `A`, and one ordered
quotient lift `Q=(z,w)`. For this fixed `Q`, at most two unordered product
representations `E` have zero normalized coupling `lambda_(E,Q)`. Hence one
of the at least nine or seven vertices in the product star has

```text

```

If `J_star` is the ideal generated by the normalized differences on that
star, the single-quotient packet is

```text

```

prime ideal, so that rational prime divides `Norm(I_17)`. No second quotient
lift and no quotient-collision spoke is needed. The quotient lift itself is
retained and certifies `R(t)>=1`.

This theorem strengthens one compiler row. It does not prove `(SQE3)`,

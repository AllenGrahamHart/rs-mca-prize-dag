# Audit

1. At `L=0`, sixteen low-excess units use the exact mass
   `(n-1)(n-2)`. The seventeenth unit is paid only on `D(t)>0`; dropping
   `S_D` would be false at an abstract profile with `P=35,D=1`.
2. The pointwise affine theorem is applied to `R(t)+1`. Replacing this by an
   energy estimate or summing over all field parameters would not prove the
   displayed `S_D` bound.
3. There are at most `n-1` diagonal targets because they are an image of
   `(1-H)\{0}`. No injectivity of squaring is assumed.
4. The high rectangle is `P>=36,R>=1`; replacing either strict threshold by
   the preceding endpoint changes the budget split.
5. The endpoint ratios equal the `E=16` ratios because both have minimum
   small-representation count `m=16`, but parity makes the `(36,1)` allowance
   strictly larger. The two retained moments are also different.
6. For fixed `Q`, the cases `z=q` and `z=-q` yield the same unordered `E`.
   The zero-coupling fiber has size at most two, not three.
7. The coupling anchor may be any vertex of the forced product star. It need
   not be the vertex originally chosen as the star center.
8. The sole quotient lift remains in the certificate. Omitting it would lose
   the target-local `R>=1` condition.
9. No quotient-collision spoke exists at this endpoint, and none is claimed.
10. A larger moment allowance and a smaller certificate packet are not a
    survivor bound.

# Nondeep tangent support-wise payment

- **status:** REFUTED
- **scope:** a universal payment outside the deep-MCA range

## Refuted claim

Let `C=RS[F,D,K]`, put `r=n-A`, and suppose a received pair `(u,v)` has a
joint codeword-pair explanation on at least `A` coordinates. Then all
support-wise MCA-bad slopes at agreement `A` fit in a tangent column of size

```text
r+1=n-A+1.
```

This is false without a hypothesis such as the deep-regime condition
`3r<=n-K`. A smooth rate-`1/4` counterexample over `F_17` has eight exact-`A`
bad slopes while `r+1=6`.

The counterexample does not address any official clean-rate candidate. It
shows that global pair proximity is not, by itself, a support-wise payment
and that the official XR assembly needs a separate nondeep mismatch theorem.

# Audit

`D` counts diagonal shifted-factor representations, not roots in the original
subgroup. The swap identity is `U=(P+D)/2`.

The exceptional count removes every diagonal and the at-most-one pair
containing `-1`, even when its coefficient norm is already small. Therefore
`(EML1)` is a lower bound and remains valid when the two exceptional classes
overlap.

The parity restriction on `D` follows from the swap identity. This is why the
uniform rich-fiber bound is `7+ceil(e/2)`, sharper than substituting only
`D<=2`.

The weak degree-four alternatives in `(EML6)` are necessary profiles, not
existence claims. Either profile may still have nine small vectors or a
weight-six center in a particular row.

The ladder controls product-fiber geometry only. It does not pay `R(t)` or
turn the critical weighted sum into an unweighted candidate count.

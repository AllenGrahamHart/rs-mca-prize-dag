# Audit

- Contracting one divided-power factor lowers the form degree by one, giving
  `2d` exactly; ordinary derivatives and characteristic assumptions are not
  imported.
- The residual matrix is the middle catalecticant and is symmetric before
  any Kronecker equivalence is chosen.
- `adj M` is nonzero because generic rank is exactly one below matrix size.
- Polynomial divisibility uses primitivity of the whole kernel vector, not
  pairwise coprimality of its coordinates.
- Degree subtraction is exact: `deg adj M=d`, `deg(q_iq_j)=2e=d-1`, so the
  common factor is linear rather than merely bounded by one.
- The unique exceptional point is projective. It may be the boundary slope;
  then the finite supported omission is necessarily zero.
- `O=1` is not automatic when the exceptional slope is supported; the
  specialized generator may still have all residual roots.
- The identity is structural, not a stratum exclusion.

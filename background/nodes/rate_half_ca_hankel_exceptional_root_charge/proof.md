# Proof

Retain all notation from
`rate_half_ca_hankel_minimal_index_budget`.  In particular, over `F(Z)` the
right and left kernels are generated by shifts of the same degree-`rho`
binary form `Q_Z`, and the regular part of the Kronecker canonical form has
size

```text
delta=rho-Ae.                                          (1)
```

Write `Q_Z=H Qbar_Z`, where `H` is the product of the `s` fixed simple
domain factors and `deg Qbar_Z=d=rho-s`.  The preceding theorem proves that
`Qbar_Z(x)` is a nonzero homogeneous polynomial of parameter degree `e` for
every `x in D\S`.  Hence the total number of pairs

```text
(gamma,x),       x in D\S,       Qbar_gamma(x)=0,
```

over all projective slopes, counted with multiplicity, is exactly
`(N-s)e`.  Restricting to finite supported slopes can only decrease this
number.

## 1. A supported exceptional slope retains domain roots

Fix a supported finite slope `gamma`, and write

```text
rank M(gamma)=rho-c_gamma,       c_gamma>=0.             (2)
```

Let `G_gamma` be the minimal homogeneous generator of the specialized
binary apolar ideal.  Since the locator degree satisfies `r<=R/2`, the other
complete-intersection generator has degree

```text
R+1-(rho-c_gamma)>r.
```

Therefore the whole degree-`r` specialized kernel consists of multiples of
`G_gamma`, and

```text
deg G_gamma=rho-c_gamma.                                (3)
```

The rank-zero specialization is read separately with `G_gamma=1`; it needs
no root charge and the formulas below give zero.  Otherwise, both the
specialized generic generator `Q_gamma` and the supported squarefree split
locator belong to the specialized apolar ideal, in degrees `rho` and `r`
respectively.  Thus `G_gamma` divides both of them.  Divisibility by the
squarefree locator shows that `G_gamma` itself is squarefree and split over
`D`.  At most `s` of its roots can lie in the fixed set `S`, so
`Qbar_gamma` has at least

```text
max(0,rho-c_gamma-s)=max(0,d-c_gamma)                   (4)
```

distinct roots in `D\S`.

When `c_gamma=0`, this recovers the `d` roots used by the regular-slope
incidence argument.  Equation `(4)` is the extra charge at rank drops.

## 2. Rank-loss multiplicities share one degree budget

Only the regular Kronecker block can lose rank under specialization.  Its
homogeneous determinant has degree `delta`.  At a slope where its rank drops
by `c_gamma`, the determinant vanishes to order at least `c_gamma`: this is
immediate from the local Smith form, or from the fact that every nonzero term
in the determinant must use at least `c_gamma` entries vanishing at that
slope.  Consequently

```text
sum_exceptional c_gamma<=delta.                         (5)
```

Let `T_reg` be the number of supported slopes with `c_gamma=0`, and let
`E` be the number with `c_gamma>0`.  Equations `(4)` and `(5)` give

```text
(N-s)e
 >= T_reg*d + sum_exceptional max(0,d-c_gamma).
```

For every `c>=0`,

```text
d<=max(0,d-c)+c.
```

Adding the rank-loss budget therefore yields

```text
(T_reg+E)d
 <=(N-s)e+sum_exceptional c_gamma
 <=(N-s)e+delta.
```

This is `(ERC1)`, and division by `d` proves `(ERC2)`.

## 3. Contracted residual charge

Assume a generic-rank supported slope exists, so the fixed domain factors in
`H` are simple.  The contracted pencil

```text
Psi_Z=H o Phi_Z
```

has generic minimal generator `Qbar_Z`, generic rank `d`, nullity sum
`A+s`, and therefore regular Kronecker size

```text
delta_res=d-(A+s)e.                                   (6)
```

At a supported exceptional slope, retain the notation `G_gamma` and
`c_gamma` above.  Put

```text
H_0=gcd(G_gamma,H),       deg H_0=s-j_gamma,
G_gamma=H_0 G_1,          H=H_0 H_1.
```

Because `G_gamma` is squarefree and split, `H_1` and `G_1` are coprime.
The divisibility `G_gamma | H Qbar_gamma` then implies

```text
G_1 | Qbar_gamma,       deg G_1=d-c_gamma+j_gamma.     (7)
```

In particular `j_gamma<=c_gamma`, and `(7)` supplies that many distinct
roots in `D\S`.

Moreover `H G_1=H_1G_gamma` belongs to `Ann(Phi_gamma)`, so

```text
G_1 in Ann(Phi_gamma):H=Ann(Psi_gamma).
```

The residual catalecticant therefore has rank at most `deg G_1`; its rank
loss `c_res(gamma)` from the generic value `d` satisfies

```text
c_res(gamma)>=c_gamma-j_gamma.                         (8)
```

Equations `(7)` and `(8)` charge at least `d` units to every supported
exceptional slope.  A supported generic-rank slope already contributes all
`d` roots.  Summing residual root incidence and local Smith multiplicities
of the residual determinant gives

```text
T*d<=(N-s)e+delta_res,
```

which is `(ERC1R)`.

## 4. Official `A=3` arithmetic

Set `rho=4m-1`, `N=4rho+4`, and `A=3`.

If no generic-rank slope is supported, every supported slope is a zero of
the regular determinant, so `T<=delta<=rho` and there is nothing to prove.
Assume henceforth that a generic-rank supported slope exists.  The residual
minimal-index bound is

```text
(s+3)e<=rho-s,
```

or equivalently `(ERC3)`.  For `1<=e<=m-1`, the right side of `(ERC2)` is at
most `rho+1`.  Here is an integer-only verification of the inequality.  The
quotient in `(ERC2)` is increasing with `s`, so use

```text
s<=(rho-3e)/(e+1).
```

After clearing `e+1`, the positive margin against `(rho+2)(rho-s)` is
bounded below by

```text
P(e)=e rho^2+(-4e^2+e-1)rho-4e^2+5e.                  (7)
```

As a function of `e`, `P` is concave.  Its values at `e=1` and
`e=m-1=(rho-3)/4` are positive, so it is positive throughout the interval.
Thus the quotient is strictly below `rho+2`, and its floor is at most
`rho+1`.

If `e>=m`, then

```text
rho-3e<=m-1<e+1.
```

Equation `(ERC3)` forces the integer `s` to be zero.  Since `3e<=rho`, one
also has `e<rho`, and `(ERC2)` simplifies to

```text
floor((Ne+rho-3e)/rho)
 =floor(4e+1+e/rho)
 =4e+1.
```

In the live branch a generic-rank supported slope exists.  Any nonconstant
parameter-independent factor of `Q_Z` would then divide a split locator, so
all of its roots would lie in `D` and it would be counted by `H`.  Thus
`s=0` makes the remaining generator primitive also with respect to fixed
factors in the binary variable.

At the strict budget the required cap is `rho+1`, so every `e<=m-1` is
closed.  At the half-distance `A=3` profile the locator degree is `rho+1`
and the required cap is `rho+2`; `(ERC4)` also closes `e=m`.  This proves all
`A=3` claims.

## 5. Official `A=1` arithmetic

Now write `h=rho=4m`, `N=4h`, and `A=1`.  As before, a profile with no
generic-rank supported slope is already paid by `T<=delta<=h`.  Otherwise
the residual minimal-index inequality and `(ERC2)` give `(ERC5)`.

For `1<=e<=m-1`, the quotient in `(ERC5)` is increasing with `s`, and

```text
s<=(h-e)/(e+1).
```

After clearing `e+1`, the positive margin against `(h+2)(h-s)` is bounded
below by

```text
P_1(e)=e h^2+(-4e^2-e-1)h+3e.                         (8)
```

This is concave in `e`.  It is positive at `e=1` and at
`e=m-1=h/4-1`, hence throughout the interval.  Thus `T<=h+1` there.

At `e=m`, the residual constraint gives `s<=2`.  Direct substitution in the
residual bound `(ERC1R)` gives

```text
s=0: T<=h,
s=1: T<=h+1,
s=2: T<=h+1.
```

For every `e>=m`, the same constraint forces `s<=2`.  Solving it separately
for the three possible values gives

```text
s=2  only if 3e<=h-2,
s=1  only if 2e<=h-1,
s=0  only if e<=h.
```

The closed boundary cases remove `e=m` from all three rows.  These are
exactly the ranges in `(ERC6)`.  QED.

## 6. Fixed-tail route fence

For completeness, the finite counterexample in the statement uses moment
vectors

```text
y_0=(40,3,89,98,65,15,26,88,4,51),
y_1=(12,61,7,71,86,77,93,67,1,0)
```

over `F_101`.  Direct convolution shows that `Q_0+ZQ_1` annihilates the
`6 x 5` Hankel matrix of `y_0+Zy_1` identically.  Row reduction at all `101`
finite slopes gives rank histogram `{4:100,3:1}`.  Hence the generic right
kernel is generated by this degree-four form, its parameter degree is one,
and `A=10+1-2*4=3`, `delta=4-3=1`.  Euclidean reduction in `F_101[X]` gives
`gcd(Q_0,Q_1)=1`.  The verifier performs each of these exact checks.

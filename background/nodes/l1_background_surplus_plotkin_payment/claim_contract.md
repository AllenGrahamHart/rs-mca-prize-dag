# Claim contract - L1 background-surplus Plotkin payment

## Inputs

- the joint cross-determinant intersection budget;
- the Plotkin/Rankin boundary and bounded-excess puncturing argument;
- exact background agreements as roots of the determined numerator;
- fixed petal polarity and the L1 lower cutoff.

## Output

Every fixed effective-excess stratum satisfies `(PS4)`, and every fixed
`(p,E_z)` box is polynomial per source chart. More strongly, every fixed
logarithmic effective-excess window `(PS5L)` is polynomial. A counterfamily
obeys `(PS6)--(PS8)`.

## Consumer rule

Consumers may discard contributors with `(E_P-4z)/log_2 n` bounded. They
must stratify by the exact background count before using equal-weight coding,
and must not enumerate the background subset as an independent choice.

## Falsifier

Two contributors violating `(PS3)`, a bounded-effective-excess stratum
exceeding `(PS4)`, or failure of `(PS8)`.

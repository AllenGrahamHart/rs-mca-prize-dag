# Audit

The orientation in `(TS2)` is load-bearing: the positive and negative
supports of an indicator difference determine `X` and `Y` separately. The
common part `I` is then unique.

Distinct support ownership is also load-bearing. It is not imposed by an
arbitrary first-match convention; it follows from the P-B global-genericity
hypothesis by recombining two distinct slopes on a common support.

The multiplicity-one cutoff `t>=K` is purely combinatorial and stronger than
Plotkin on all six official terminal intervals. Two residuals in one fiber
would give two family members sharing the fixed `t`-set `X`, contradicting
the low-core cap `K-1`.

The strict Plotkin denominator is sharp in a small fixture. For all
two-subsets of a four-set with `H=1`, the fiber

```text
X={0},       Y={1},       R_(X,Y)={{2},{3}}
```

has size two. Here `N-2t=2<4H`, and `(TS4)` also gives two. A mutation to
the bound one is rejected by `verify_audit.py`. In the same fixture
`K=2` and `t=K-1`, so it also proves that the multiplicity-one cutoff cannot
be lowered from `K` to `K-1` for abstract low-core families.

The theorem pays multiplicity and energy only. Counting narrow difference
types, or inferring a slope bound from low wide-shift energy, would be an
invalid strengthening.

Do not identify `(TS2)` with CAP25's local primitive shift-pair degree.
CAP25 counts distinct locator neighbors of one support; `(TS2)` counts how
many ordered support pairs realize one already fixed indicator difference.

# Audit

1. The vanishing-sum polynomial has nonnegative coefficients because every
   minus sign is absorbed into the root `-1 in mu_N`.
2. The coefficient-balancing proof uses `N` being a power of two, so
   `Phi_N=X^(N/2)+1`.
3. Pairwise distinct squares excludes both equality and antipodality among
   `1,x,y,w`.
4. The proof is characteristic-zero only. Small finite-field harmonic
   witnesses are positive controls, not contradictions.
5. The official lift order is `N=2d=2^40`, not the square order `d=2^39`.
6. Six separate inverse equations keep the sparse presentation cubic; one
   inverse of their product would have degree thirteen.
7. A unit ideal over `Q` supplies a finite bad-characteristic certificate,
   not an exclusion before `Delta_H` is computed and factored.

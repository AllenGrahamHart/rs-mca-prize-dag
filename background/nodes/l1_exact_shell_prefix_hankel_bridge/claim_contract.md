# Claim contract - L1 exact-shell prefix/Hankel bridge

## Inputs

- interpolation on finite sets of distinct field points;
- one admissible source layout for the local chart statement;
- the exact core-defect and saturated Hankel-map definitions.

## Output

- a disjoint exact-shell decomposition of `ImgFib_U(s)`;
- an exact first-owner removal of raw sub-support multiplicity;
- a unitriangular equivalence between each received-word prefix zero shell and
  its fixed-source Hankel chart.

## Consumer rule

There are two valid routes from this node:

1. prove a row-sharp theorem for the primitive exact received-word shells and
   pay the quotient shells separately;
2. bound and aggregate the saturated Hankel zero fibers directly inside the
   fixed source.

Do not cite upstream locator Q, a toy power-sum census, or the special F2
Myerson statement as route 1 without an explicit map/owner transport theorem.

## Nonclaims

No exact shell is bounded here. No quotient-periodic shell is classified.
The unitriangular matrix depends on the retained core and preserves the zero
target; it is not asserted to identify complete nonzero fiber distributions
across different charts.

## Falsifier

A listed codeword missing from `(EH3)`, a raw higher-agreement sub-support
surviving `(EH2)`, or a source-chart example where one side of `(EH6)--(EH7)`
vanishes and the other does not.

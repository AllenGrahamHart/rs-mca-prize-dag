# Upstream crosswalk - fixed-cofactor prefix transport

## Exact locator-Q overlap

At the top shell `a=deg U`, the error cofactor is a scalar. The shell is
exactly one prescribed-prefix divisor fiber, with no residual exactness
deletion. This is a proved object-level identification with upstream locator
Q, although the needed row-sharp bound remains open in both programs.

## Bounded cofactor

For positive `e<k`, choosing the cofactor determines a depth-`w+e` locator
prefix. The `q^e` possible cofactors are exactly offset by those `e` extra
coordinates under an ambient-normalized row-sharp theorem. Under upstream's
primitive image normalization, however, the exact remaining factor is the
effective-image collapse `q^(w+e)/L_(a,w+e)`. See
`l1_cofactor_depth_budget_cancellation`. The companion
`l1_cofactor_prefix_pade_graph_normal_form` shows that the targets form one
explicit codimension-`w` graph rather than an arbitrary union.

## Growing cofactor

The current finite row-sharp Q problem is posed at one active degree and
depth `w`, not uniformly over `(a,w+e)`. Moreover, at the deployed rows the
deeper ambient image becomes sparse after `e=1` or `e=2`; image-normalized Q
then leaves an unaffordable occupation factor. This is the part naturally
assigned to upstream's arbitrary-word list-interior term, split-divisor/Pade-
graph transversality, or a collective Toeplitz theorem. Per-cofactor Q at the
active depth is not that theorem.

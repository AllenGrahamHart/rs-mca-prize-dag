# Audit

The factors two and four in `(DSM1),(DSM2)` pay distance-four edges inside
the weighted centroid ledger `W=2N_4+N_6`; they are not multiplicities of the
distance-six count.

The antipodal overlap allowance eight includes both the six generic overlap
edges and the two possible edges incident to the norm-one antipodal vertex.
The remaining edges are therefore generic--generic with six disjoint signed
support atoms, exactly the DSP8 split-pencil stratum.

The two antipodal edges in `(DSM3)` need not share a vertex. The cross
generator in `(DSM5)` is load-bearing when they do not: two unrelated
collision ideals can vanish at different product targets under one row root.

The pure-star degree bounds use averages only after the complete pointwise
edge lower bounds. They do not assert regularity or classify the stars.

A failure to factor or enumerate these ideals is an algorithmic limitation,
not evidence against the theorem. A large computation remains unauthorized
until a finite template generator and measured cost ceiling exist.

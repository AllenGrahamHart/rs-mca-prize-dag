# Result

Every retained near-third HGE4 pair is a Kummer-normalized three-member
divisor pencil:

```text
S-ay^h | 1-y^m,       S | 1-kappa y^m,       S+ay^h | 1-y^m.
```

Primitivity forces the midpoint to split over the base field at every width:
any nontrivial Kummer factor degree would induce a common scaling stabilizer
of the two outside members. This is the correct candidate and certificate
interface for the retained near-third strip; extension-field midpoint
branches and the full binary-necklace class are avoidable overcounts.
Its endpoint coefficients feed the proved trace-power gate, which makes the
midpoint Kummer scalar an explicit one-dimensional candidate test.

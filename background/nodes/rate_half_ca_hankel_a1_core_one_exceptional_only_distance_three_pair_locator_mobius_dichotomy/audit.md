# Audit

- Distinct monic quadratics cannot span dimension one.
- The reducible bidegree-`(1,1)` relation would force every disjoint pair to
  contain one common point, so two pairs exclude it.
- Odd characteristic is supplied by the official prime-field collapse.
- The verifier checks antipodal and genuinely fractional involutions, their
  trace-zero matrix squares, a generic rank-three family, and a one-pair
  mutation that breaks the common involution line.

# Claim contract - decorated shift-pair gcd descent and sharpness

## Inputs

- one exact decorated shift-pair record;
- its canonical cofactor gcd;
- the primitive uniqueness theorem at `e<=w`.

## Outputs

- canonical reduction `(e,w)->(e-c,w+c)`;
- routing of domain common-factor roots into the common agreement core;
- a ten-member exact-shell family proving support-only sharpness at `e=w+1`.

## Consumer rule

Apply gcd descent before classifying decorations. Forget the reduced
decoration only when `e-c<=w+c`. Retain the received-word/Toeplitz owner in
the complementary high-cofactor branch.

## Nonclaims

The common gcd is not paid here. The sharpness family varies the received
word and does not produce ten pairs in one list. L1 remains unrefuted.

## Falsifier

A gcd descent violating `(GD3)` or `(GD4)`, a domain root of `D` outside `G`,
or failure of exactness/coprimality in one of the ten printed witnesses.

# Audit

1. The published theorem is applied on the smooth normalization, not on a
   possibly singular affine model.
2. Every subgroup point is outside the zeros and poles of `x,y` and
   contributes at least one common-zero multiplicity after lifting.
3. The genus bound is the arithmetic genus `(3-1)(3-1)=4` in
   `P^1 x P^1`; singularities can only decrease it.
4. The support bound `|S|<=12` counts zeros and poles of both degree-three
   coordinate maps without assuming disjointness.
5. Multiplicative dependence is excluded from the permanent
   two-dimensional Newton support, not from a random coefficient test.
6. Both terms in the positive-characteristic maximum are paid. The very
   large official characteristic makes the second term negligible, but it
   is not omitted.
7. This theorem supersedes the unpaid `5376` Stepanov route only for the
   Laurent-end branch; the earlier route fence remains mathematically
   accurate.

# Audit - L1 fixed-source anchored triple-polarity closure

## Checked axes

1. Quotient partitions are counted up to additive shift.
2. One whole fixed-source petal is required as an anchor.
3. Complete fibers need not cover the domain; residual points are charged in
   both layout and defect polarity.
4. Partial source cores are allowed.
5. Source members are unique from core-plus-petal agreements.
6. A central-polynomial relabelling is not a distinct canonical chart.
7. Four orientation vectors produce the conservative `16^L` factor.
8. Strict-window and thin-edge CRT bounds cover every degree strip.
9. Numerator/Forney multiplicity is paid, not merely renamed as a key.
10. Growing polarity, unanchored refinements, and arbitrary petals remain
    explicit.

## Remaining attack

Prove an anchor-or-refinement theorem for internal quotient maps, then pay
the growing signed layout/defect/petal polarity. Separately handle cells whose
petal locators do not lie in one common constant-shift pencil.

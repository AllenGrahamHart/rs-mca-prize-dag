# Proof

Let `A=(1-H)\{0}`, so `|A|=n-1`, and recall that `R(t)` counts ordered
ratios `d/c=t` in `A`. The total nonidentity quotient mass is

```text
sum_(t!=1)R(t)=(n-1)(n-2).                         (1)
```

Fix one `c_0 in A`. As `d` ranges over `A\{c_0}`, the `n-2` ratios `d/c_0`
are distinct and nonidentity. Hence the nonidentity support of `R` has size
at least `n-2`. Subtracting one from every positive fiber in `(1)` gives

```text
T_1
 =sum_(t!=1)R(t)-#{t!=1:R(t)>0}
 <=(n-1)(n-2)-(n-2)=(n-2)^2,
```

which proves `(ADC1)` for `L=1`. Since `rho_L<=rho_1`, it proves the displayed
bound for every `L>=1`.

Write `e(t)=(P(t)-18)_+`. The cutoff-18 double-accident identity is

```text
X_18=A_18+Y_18,
A_18<= (n-1)^2,
Y_18=sum_(t!=1)e(t)rho_1(t).                       (2)
```

For any `L>=1`, split instead

```text
R(t)=min(R(t),L)+rho_L(t).                          (3)
```

The first part contributes at most

```text
sum_(t!=1)e(t)min(R(t),L)
 <=L sum_t P(t)=L(n-1)^2.                          (4)
```

The part using `rho_L` and satisfying `e(t)<=E` is at most `E T_L`. Equations
`(ADC1),(3),(4)` show that C36' follows once

```text
17 sum_(t!=1,e(t)>E)e(t)rho_L(t)<=B_(L,E)(n).      (5)
```

This proves `(ADC2),(ADC3)`. If `L=17-E`, direct expansion gives

```text
B_(17-E,E)(n)
 =11n^2+(578+34E)n-(289+51E)>11n^2                (6)
```

on every official order. The retained integer conditions are
`P>=19+E,R>=L+1=18-E`. The existing product-degree ladder gives the five
certificate strengths in `(ADC4)`. If any one rectangle is empty, the left
side of `(5)` vanishes, proving the stated exclusion criterion and `(ADC5)`.

Take `(L,E)=(3,14)`. The proved antipodal-tail distance-six split gives
pointwise

```text
e(t)<=(16/53)N_6(t)  on the antipodal-free class,
e(t)<=(8/21)N_6(t)   on the antipodal class.       (7)
```

Multiplying `(7)` by `17rho_3(t)` and summing gives

```text
17Y_(3)^>14
 <=(136/1113)(42Mbar_(3),6,33^0+53Mbar_(3),6,33^A). (8)
```

Thus `(ADC6)` implies `(5)` and C36'. Moreover `(6)` at `E=14` gives

```text
B_(3,14)(n)=11n^2+1054n-1003>11n^2.               (9)
```

Substituting the smaller right side `11n^2` into `(ADC6)` and dividing by
`136*42` gives `(ADC7)`. Repeating with `L=1` gives
`B_(1,14)>45n^2` and the wider constant `2385/272`.

For the direct form, take `E=6` in `(5)`. The lower-cutoff split gives

```text
17Y_(L)^>6
 <=(4/5)(10Mbar_(L),6,25^0+17Mbar_(L),6,25^A),    (10)
```

where these complete moments use `rho_L` instead of `R`. Therefore it is
sufficient that

```text
4(10Mbar_(L),6,25^0+17Mbar_(L),6,25^A)
 <=5B_(L,6)(n).                                    (11)
```

Split each complete moment into disjoint and overlapping edges. The two
global overlap covers contain at most `2n` generic--generic edges, and
`rho_L(t)<=R(t)<(51/16)n^(2/3)`. At most two additional edges meet the
antipodal representation. Consequently the proved overlap calculation,
with `rho` in place of `R`, gives

```text
Obar_(L),0+(17/10)Obar_(L),A
 <(867/80)n^(5/3)+(17/5)Sbar_(L),A.               (12)
```

The primitive shift-pair normalization gives `Kbar_(L),c=2Dbar_(L),c`.
Substitute `(12)` into `(11)` and multiply out to obtain

```text
10Kbar_(L),25^0+17Kbar_(L),25^A
 +68Sbar_(L),A+(867/4)n^(5/3)
 <=(5/2)B_(L,6)(n),                                (13)
```

which is exactly `(ADC8)` after expanding `(ADC2)`.

The antipodal mass satisfies

```text
Sbar_(L),A<=S_A<(51/32)(n-2)n^(2/3).              (14)
```

Use `(14)`, `(n-1)^2<n^2`, `(n-2)^2<n^2`,
`(n-2)n^(2/3)<n^(5/3)`, and `n^(1/3)>20`. The right side available for the
primitive-SP term in `(ADC8)` is strictly greater than

```text
(750-85L/2-255-2601/160)n^2
 =((76599-6800L)/160)n^2.                          (15)
```

Thus `(ADC9)` implies `(ADC8)` for `1<=L<=11`; the endpoint numerator is
`76599-6800*11=1799`.

Finally, a positive summand of `(ADC6)` has `P>=33` and `rho_3>0`, hence
`R>=4`. The disjoint-six multiplicity gate supplies a pure star of degree
seven or five according to target class. Fix its center `E`. The nonzero
coupling theorem says that among the quotient lifts of this target at most
one has zero

```text
lambda_(E,Q)=(beta_E C_Q-D_Q)/pi.
```

Choose four distinct lifts `Q_0,...,Q_3`; at least three couplings are nonzero,
so relabel to make `lambda_0` nonzero. Let `J_E` be generated by every
normalized star difference `(beta_F-beta_E)/pi^2`. The proved coupling-batch
odd-saturation theorem, applied to the integral ideal `J_E`, gives

```text
(J_E,lambda_0,lambda_1,lambda_2,lambda_3)O[1/2]
 =(J_E,lambda_0,theta_(0,1),theta_(0,2),theta_(0,3))O[1/2].
```

Every displayed generator vanishes in the same degree-one row-prime ideal.
That prime lies above an odd rational prime, so the localization equality
preserves its valuation. Ideal-norm divisibility proves `(ADC10)` and the
final assertion. QED.

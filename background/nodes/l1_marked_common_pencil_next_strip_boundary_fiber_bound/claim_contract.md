# Claim contract - L1 marked common-pencil next-strip boundary fiber bound

## Inputs

- fixed polarity `p<=P_0` with `ell>2P_0`;
- one common constant-shift pencil containing all selected dense petals;
- the thin boundary `d+p>=(m+1)ell` in strip `m`;
- exact list threshold and strict background remainder `r<ell`.

## Output

For fixed supports, marks, and defect locator, at most `q^p` numerators.

## Consumer rule

Consumers may combine this with the strict-window `q^(2p)` theorem and use
`q^(2P_0)` uniformly over the whole strip. They must still count supports,
defect locators, and source charts.

## Falsifier

A thin-edge contributor with `t<=m`, or more than `q^(v-g+1)` numerators for
fixed exact support, marks, and defect locator.

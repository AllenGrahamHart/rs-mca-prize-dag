# Proof

Every shifted factor `1-zeta_n^a` is divisible by `pi`. Hence a shifted
product `beta_E` is divisible by `pi^2`. It follows immediately that
`alpha_F` and `alpha_G` are algebraic integers. Both cross-products in the
numerator of `theta` are divisible by `pi^2`, and both terms in the numerator
of `lambda` are divisible by `pi`. Thus all four generators in `(DAJ2)` lie
in `O`.

The first two generators are nonzero because distinct unordered root pairs
have distinct shifted products in characteristic zero. If `theta=0`, then

```text
D_0/C_0=D_1/C_1.                                   (1)
```

The factors `C_i,D_i` are nonzero and `u_i!=v_i`, so both quotients are
nonidentity. The quotient consequence of shifted-product Sidonicity says
that a nonidentity quotient has at most one ordered representation. Equation
`(1)` would therefore give `Q_0=Q_1`, contrary to `(DAJ1)`. In particular,
`K^DA` is a nonzero ideal.

Now suppose `(DAJ4)` holds on a row with primitive subgroup generator `g`.
Since `P(t)>=19`, the proved low-distance ideal-star router supplies a rooted
star `(E;F,G)` satisfying `(ISR2)` whose three shifted products all reduce to
`t`. Since `R(t)>=2`, choose two distinct ordered quotient representations

```text
(1-g^(v_i))/(1-g^(u_i))=t,       i=0,1.             (2)
```

The condition `t!=1` gives `u_i!=v_i`, so these are admissible quotient
lifts.

Let

```text
mathfrak p=(p,zeta_n-g).
```

This is a degree-one prime ideal with norm `p`. The two product differences
reduce to zero at `zeta_n=g`. Cross-multiplying the two equations in `(2)`
shows that the numerator of `theta` also reduces to zero. Finally the first
equation in `(2)` and `beta_E mod mathfrak p=t` give

```text
beta_E C_0-D_0=0 mod mathfrak p.                    (3)
```

Because `g!=1` and `p` is odd, `pi=1-zeta_n` is a unit modulo `mathfrak p`.
Dividing the four vanishing numerators by the displayed powers of `pi`
therefore preserves membership in `mathfrak p`. Thus

```text
K^DA_(E;F,G;Q_0,Q_1) subset mathfrak p.             (4)
```

Norm reverses ideal inclusion, so `(4)` gives

```text
p=N(mathfrak p) divides N(K^DA).                    (5)
```

Each principal ideal generated by one of the four elements in `(DAJ2)` is
contained in `K^DA`. Consequently `N(K^DA)` divides each absolute principal
norm. The ideal-star router already proves

```text
|Norm(alpha_F)|,|Norm(alpha_G)|<=6^(n/4)/4.
```

Combining this with `(5)` proves `(DAJ5)`.

If `Y_18>0`, one of its integer summands has `P(t)>=19` and `R(t)>=2` for
some `t!=1`. Therefore `(DAJ4)` applies and proves `(DAJ6)`. Conversely, if
`p` is outside `D_n^DA`, no such summand exists, so `Y_18=0`. The proved
double-accident reduction then yields C36'.

Odd Galois dilation sends `pi` to a unit multiple of `pi` and sends each
displayed generator to a unit multiple of the corresponding generator for
the dilated tuple. Internal exchanges do not change a shifted product, and
exchanging `F,G` exchanges `alpha_F,alpha_G`. These operations preserve the
ideal norm. QED.

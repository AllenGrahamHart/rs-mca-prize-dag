# Audit

The theorem needs three logically separate congruences at one degree-one
prime ideal:

1. the three product representations agree;
2. the two quotient representations agree;
3. the common product and quotient values agree.

The product generators enforce the first item and `theta` enforces the
second. Only `lambda` enforces the third. An ideal containing just the first
three generators is not a valid router for `Y_18`.

Normalization removes only universal powers of `pi`. Since the row prime is
odd and the selected primitive root is not one, `pi` is a unit at the row
prime ideal; normalization neither loses nor creates an official odd-prime
congruence.

The candidate set is an outer envelope. Divisibility of one joint ideal norm
does not by itself exhibit a valid official row or a positive `Y_18` summand.
Only the forward coverage implication is used.

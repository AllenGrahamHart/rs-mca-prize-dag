# Audit

1. The partial-fraction polynomial is `Phi(W)=product(W-w_i)`, while the
   pencil factors are `B+w_iV`; this is why the first identity in `(1)` has a
   minus sign.
2. The Wronskian has degree at most `2r-2`, not the naive `2r-1`, because its
   leading terms cancel.
3. `3H-1` is even.  This is required for the displayed plus projection
   `K(z)+K(-z)`.
4. Formula `(BSP4)` uses multiplier roots `x`; the polynomial is evaluated at
   `x^(-1)`.
5. Equality in the support bound requires `J(+/-1)!=0`; otherwise its roots
   overlap the two forced zeros and the support is larger.
6. The compact formula is a theorem, not permission to materialize an
   official-degree `B,C`, or `J`.

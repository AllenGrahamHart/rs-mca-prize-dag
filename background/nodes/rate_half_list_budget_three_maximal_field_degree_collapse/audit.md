# Audit

- The theorem is restricted to `n=2^41`; `B*=3` alone does not force the
  same characteristic congruence on smaller domains.
- The extension-degree bound uses exact 2-adic LTE and the strict field cap,
  not a floating-point logarithm.
- The cubic interval has one candidate. Its exclusion needs only the exact
  divisor seven, not a probabilistic primality test or a factorization oracle.
- The quadratic conclusion is modulo `2^40`, correctly retaining all four
  square roots of one modulo `2^41`.
- Prime or quadratic field scope is not a split-pencil exclusion.

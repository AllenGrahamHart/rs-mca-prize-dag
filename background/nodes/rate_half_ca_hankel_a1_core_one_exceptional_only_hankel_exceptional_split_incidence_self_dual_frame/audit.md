# Audit

1. Division by `z` uses the proved simple exceptional root; merely knowing
   `Q(0;a)=0` would not make the constant coefficient of `g_a` nonzero.
2. Exact parameter degree and saturation make the leading coefficient and
   all `e-1` ordinary roots nonzero and distinct.
3. The generator drops the zero row `q_0|_(R_A)` and retains exactly
   `q_1,...,q_e`.
4. Ordinary replication counts exceptional incidences, not complementary
   ordinary roots; equation `(1)` fixes the orientation.
5. The final sentence identifies a sufficient next theorem, not a claim that
   arbitrary incidence designs satisfying the replication are impossible.
6. The `e=3,F_101` fixture checks nonzero diagonal self-dual weights exactly;
   it does not satisfy or test the official Forney and smooth-domain data.

# Proof

At an exceptional root `a`, the specialization `Q(0;a)` vanishes because
`q_0=A`. Exact row saturation says `Q(z;a)` has degree `e` and exactly `e`
distinct supported parameter roots. The exceptional root is simple by
kernel-plane transversality, so division by `z` gives a degree-`e-1`
squarefree polynomial with nonzero constant term. Its remaining roots are
ordinary supported slopes and are exactly the incidences in `(HSF2)`. This
proves `(HSF1)--(HSF2)` and the coefficient assertions.

Evaluation of `q_0` on `R_A` is zero. Consequently the exceptional
evaluation of the coefficient plane is generated by the rows
`q_1|_(R_A),...,q_e|_(R_A)`, which is exactly `(HSF3)`. The self-dual
evaluation-code theorem proves its rank and `(HSF4)`, as well as the
complementary-minor law.

For an ordinary clean slope `gamma`, the number of exceptional roots in its
fiber is

```text
a_gamma=#{a in R_A:Q(gamma;a)=0}
       =#{a in R_A:gamma in Z_a}.                    (1)
```

The sharp-ceiling theorem proves that the multiset of these `4e`
replications is either constant `(e-1)/2` or the one-swap multiset `(HSF5)`.
This gives the two frame classes and proves the stated reduction. QED.

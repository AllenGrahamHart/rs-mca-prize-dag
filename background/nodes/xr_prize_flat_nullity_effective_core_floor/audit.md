# Audit

- The proof truncates every residual zero set to exactly `kappa+h` points.
  Distinctness survives because the pairwise intersection cap is `kappa`.
- Minimal dense-core extraction is performed on the truncated family, so no
  exact-agreement assumption is hidden.
- The core and private-point bounds are proved directly and do not import the
  uniform split-pencil trade theorem into nonuniform cells.
- The over-budget hypothesis is used only to make the whole family satisfy
  the density inequality; `8n^3` is far larger than `2R/h` on every row.
- `kappa=a+u+v` is an effective post-persistent core, not selector rank.
- P-B has intersection cap `k-1` and is not claimed here.
- No existence at the floor and no cross-cell summation is asserted.
- No Modal or large local computation is used.

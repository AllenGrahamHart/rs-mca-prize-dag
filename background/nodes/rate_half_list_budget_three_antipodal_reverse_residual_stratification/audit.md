# Audit

The degree in `(RRS4)` is exact, not an upper bound. Exactness uses the first
nonzero centered coefficient and the nonzero leading coefficient of `V`; a
mere divisibility statement for the reverse contact would not suffice.

The characteristic hypothesis is load-bearing twice: differentiation must
lower the order `qh` by one, and `qh<d` must remain visible rather than become
a Frobenius power.

The exceptional-root set counts distinct roots. A repeated root contributes
once to `(RRS5)` regardless of multiplicity. The theorem does not claim that
every root of `T` is exceptional.

The linear and quadratic conclusions apply only when `deg V` attains the
printed minimum of its stratum. Above that floor the residual degree grows by
`q` for each added degree of `V`.

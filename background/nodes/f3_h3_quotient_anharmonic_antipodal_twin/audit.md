# Audit

- The quotient symmetry uses ordered triples `(1,z,w)` and is valid for every
  `t notin {0,1}`; collisions among the six printed orbit values cause no
  problem.
- The antipodal product bijection is ordered. It therefore proves the exact
  `P` identity without a diagonal or factor-two correction.
- `tau` preserves the antipodal class because `1-a^2` maps to `1-a^(-2)`.
- The parity improvement is nonvacuous: at `F_193`, order 64, an antipodal
  target has `P=26`. The theorem changes its effective cutoff from 25 to 26;
  it does not delete the antipodal class.
- Signed-support disjointness is not preserved. In the exact `F_97`, order-32
  control, `t=23` and `tau(t)=76` both have `(P,R)=(6,9)`, but their disjoint
  edge counts are respectively `0` and `1`. Any use that merges those edge
  ledgers is false.

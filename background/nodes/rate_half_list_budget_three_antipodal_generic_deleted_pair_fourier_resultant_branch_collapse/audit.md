# Audit

The integer resultant uses only primitive `N`th roots. Its nonvanishing is
proved from the two-zero and equal-cell conditions; it is not assumed from a
numerical norm calculation.

The split and nonsplit valuations are different. When `p=-1 mod N`,
Frobenius doubles the primitive zero block and improves `4N^2` to `2N`.
No such doubling is available when `p=1 mod N`, even if the ambient field is
quadratic.

The base-field descent uses the stronger surviving congruence
`p=1 mod 2^40=4N`, not merely `p=1 mod N`. It descends the quotient-pencil
data, not the full order-`2^41` evaluation domain.

The theorem begins only after the complete canonical factorization. A packet
rejected by a primary, secondary, span, splitting, or Möbius gate does not
produce the two signed root cells used here.

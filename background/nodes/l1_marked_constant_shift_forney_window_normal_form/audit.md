# Audit - L1 marked constant-shift Forney-window normal form

## Checked axes

1. Module determinant degree is the quotient length `t`; pointwise
   divisibility alone gives only `Q|det`.
2. The reduced-basis predictable-degree property is needed for both
   `mu+nu=t` and the exact truncated dimension.
3. Exact saturation excludes `nu>m`; raw interpolation modules can and do
   have a second basis degree above the strip.
4. The evaluated multipliers, not their formal bivariate presentations, are
   the objects whose gcd divides `J`.
5. The construction realizes every admissible `(mu,nu)`, including `mu=0`;
   empty products are interpreted as one.
6. The theorem classifies only common constant-shift locators and does not
   supply aggregate first-match multiplicity.

## Remaining attack

Identify which Forney strata are already quotient/profile-owned, and prove a
uniform first-match count for every unowned coefficient-multiplier family.

# Audit

The exponent at the exceptional slope is `2e`, not `e`: all `2e` roots of
`A` contain that simple parameter root. At an ordinary slope, the resultant
multiplicity is the exceptional intersection `a_z`, not the complement size
`w_z`.

In the swapped profile, `z_min` names the minimum **complement** and therefore
has the larger intersection exponent `k_0+1`. The factor `z_max` has exponent
`k_0-1` and appears in the cleared denominator side of `(QER4)`.

The resultant has degree `2e^2` in `z`: `2e+4e k_0=2e^2`. This is an exact
factorization statement, not authorization to form that dense polynomial.
A useful implementation must preserve `P_ord` and the two optional linear
factors succinctly.

The complementary polynomial `H_A=P_X/A` is exactly the factor-descent
polynomial `H=A_1(0;X)`. Thus `(QER5)--(QER6)` describe the cross-resultant
between the exceptional complement and the moving clean locator. It has no
exceptional `z` factor: the full exponent `r-1=2e` is already exhausted by
the `2e` roots of `A`.

# Audit

1. `F(X^4)` is not treated as a separate order-four matching. Its deck
   group contains the base-field involution `X|->-X`, which is enough for
   divisor invariance.
2. The bootstrap comparison pair need not initially be a deck orbit. Its two
   roots are paid explicitly, as are the four possible roots of `P_m`.
3. The at-most-two fixed-point payment applies only to `X|->c/X`.
4. Once one exact deck orbit is found, a tail mate cannot land in that fixed
   comparison pair; this removes the two-root bootstrap payment and recovers
   the sharp `6/8` bounds.
5. The quartic `P_m` is nonzero and is nonvanishing on every other
   exceptional pair by the pair-crossing gate.
6. The antiweight-derived branch uses invariant `r_l` directly and therefore
   has no comparison-quartic escape.
7. The downstream row-codegree and Pade proofs depend on the involution
   interface, not on the degree of the larger common field.

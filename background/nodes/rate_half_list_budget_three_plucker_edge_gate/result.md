# Result

The selected B*=3 algebra now has a bounded first gate. Before any large
subgroup block can occur, six polynomials of degree at most two must satisfy
one degree-at-most-four Plucker identity. Passing that gate, all four large
locators lie in a rank-two rational pencil generated by two of them, and the
entire domain factorization is one exact equation with an exceptional factor
of degree at most eight.

This gives a disciplined attack order: classify the six finite edge-degree
patterns, then apply subgroup algebra only to surviving two-generator
factorizations. The classification remains open.

# Audit

- The candidate uses rational norms, not one selected degree-one prime. Thus
  it covers every embedding `zeta |-> g` of the unique order-`n` subgroup.
- Distinct unordered pairs have distinct shifted products in characteristic
  zero, so no zero norm enters `(LDR1)`.
- The cutoff is `P>=19`: swap orbits then supply ten unordered
  representations, exactly the input used by the proved packing lemma.
- Membership in `C_n` is only necessary for richness. Candidate prime factors
  may be spurious and must be checked on the actual finite-field row.
- Factoring an incomplete subset of orbit norms is evidence, not a fixed-order
  certificate. Completeness requires an orbit census and factor certificates.
- The `530,590,075` lower bound counts Galois/exchange orbits, not distinct
  absolute norms. It fences raw enumeration but leaves symbolic template
  compression open.

# Audit

- `(FO1)` is proved on the whole common support, not only on its first
  circuit.
- The spanning owner uses a basis contained in `S`, so every active block
  contains that basis.
- The moving-zero cap is `floor((R-v)/h)`. Replacing it by `floor(R/h)` loses
  the loop-defect improvement.
- The proper-flat branch uses closure in the coordinate matroid of `W`; it
  does not claim that `b,q` extend to all of that closure.
- The result is an owner dichotomy, not an aggregate basis/flat census.
- No Modal or large local computation is used.

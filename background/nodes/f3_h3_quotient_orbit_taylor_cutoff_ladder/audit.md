# Audit

1. The cutoff index `c` corresponds to multiplicity at least `c+1` because
   Hasse derivatives are numbered from zero.
2. Prime supports descend as `c` increases. The squarefree divisibility in
   `(TCL4)` has the same direction.
3. Cutoff two removes different-root resultant false positives but retains
   genuine product multiplicities from three through thirty-five. Exact row
   filtering remains mandatory.
4. The lower auxiliary degree does not bound coefficient size, resultant
   time, content extraction, factorization cost, or candidate inflation.
5. Every canonical block must be processed. Partial cutoff ladders give only
   partial evidence.
6. The theorem compares prime radicals, not valuations or principal ideals.

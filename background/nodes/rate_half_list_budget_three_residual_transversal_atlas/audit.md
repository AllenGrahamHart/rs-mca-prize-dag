# Audit

- Every root cap is for a fixed constant pencil and counts only roots not
  already consumed by its displayed pairwise-coprime members.
- The `K_4-e` and `K_4` base-pencil conclusions count degree-`d-2` members;
  a leading-coefficient cancellation of smaller degree is not silently
  counted as a degree-`d-2` member.
- The cross-ratio fibers have degree at most two. Their nonconstancy follows
  from disjoint edge roots, not from a genericity assumption.
- The pendant argument works in all three surviving degree chambers. It uses
  only `e_13|b_13`, `deg b_13<=2`, and the distinct `13,03,12` edge roots.
- The exceptional-point counts are respectively `4,5,4,2,3` for a
  `K_4-e` triangle, a `K_4` triangle, the triangle-singleton degree-`d-1`
  pencil, its degree-`d` pencil, and the pendant pencil.
- The exact counts apply to all thirteen deficit-free pencils after importing
  the two path counts. They do not count all rational pencils in the witness.
- The official value `d=2^39` exceeds every printed threshold.
- No claim is made that an explicit primitive map cannot occur on the
  official subgroup. That is the remaining arithmetic problem.

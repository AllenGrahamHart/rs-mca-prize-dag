# Proof

Let `u_i` be representatives of the two received-word cosets and let
`s_i=Hu_i`. A line word `u_0+gamma u_1` agrees with a codeword outside an
error set `E` exactly when its syndrome belongs to the column span `V_E`:

```text
H(u_0+gamma u_1)=s_0+gamma s_1 in V_E.              (1)
```

The same support admits a mutual explanation by two codewords exactly when
both received components can be corrected inside `E`, equivalently

```text
s_0 in V_E and s_1 in V_E.                          (2)
```

A support-wise MCA witness is a line agreement for which `(2)` fails.
Taking `|E|<=r` is the same as agreement at least `n-r=a`, so `(1)` and the
negation of `(2)` prove `(SL1)`.

Adding codewords to `u_0` or `u_1` leaves their syndromes unchanged. Every
syndrome has a received-word representative because a full-rank
parity-check map is onto. Hence syndrome pairs classify all received lines
modulo `C^2`, and the search is exhaustive.

For `(SL2)`, use the following points, listed by slope:

```text
gamma       0  1  2  3  4  5  6
x_gamma     1  2  3  4  5  1  1
y_gamma     6  6  6  6  6  4  5
```

They are nonzero, distinct, and sum to `gamma` in `F_7`. Expanding the right
side of `(SL3)` gives `(0,1,x_gamma+y_gamma)=(0,1,gamma)`, proving combined
membership in `V_{x_gamma,y_gamma}`. The vectors in this two-column span
whose first coordinate is zero form the one-dimensional line generated by
the column difference. Since `s_0` and `s_1` are independent and both have
first coordinate zero, they cannot both lie in that span. Thus every slope
is bad.

The verifier constructs every `V_E` with `|E|<=2`, checks these seven
certificates, and independently enumerates all `7^6` syndrome pairs to obtain
maximum bad-slope count seven. The Vandermonde parity-check matrix has every
three columns independent, so its kernel is a length-six, dimension-three
MDS code, equivalent up to coordinate scaling to a rate-half generalized
Reed-Solomon code. QED.

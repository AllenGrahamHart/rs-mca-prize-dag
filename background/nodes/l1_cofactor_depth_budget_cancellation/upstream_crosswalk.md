# Upstream crosswalk - cofactor-depth budget cancellation

## Q terminology

Use upstream's distinction exactly:

- **ambient normalization:** divisor mass divided by the full formal prefix
  codomain `q^d`;
- **image normalization:** divisor mass divided by the attained prefix image
  size `L_(a,d)`;
- **effective-image collapse:** the penalty `q^d/L_(a,d)`.

The cofactor union cancels at ambient scale.  Under primitive image-
normalized Q, it leaves the effective-image collapse factor.  This gives a
direct list-interior reason for retaining upstream's full-image certificate.

## Finite row-sharp Q

Upstream `prob:row-sharp-q` is a pruned atom at the deployed adjacent degree
and depth `w`.  L1 needs degrees `a` across all exact shells and depths
`w+e`.  Porting the active Q theorem, once proved, therefore requires a
printed degree/depth/owner transport; it cannot be inferred from the current
F2 ladder/tower identity.

## Contributor value

A useful upstream computation should measure, for each deeper profile, all
three of

```text
maximum occupied fiber, attained image size, occupied cofactor targets.
```

Reporting only max/mean over attained targets misses the factor that decides
whether the cofactor union fits the finite row budget. The Pade graph normal
form further requires reporting the split-divisor intersection with the
realized graph, rather than treating occupied targets as an unstructured set.

# Result

The primitive antipodal quartic residual is now also degree-balanced. Its
unique lower-leading-degree pencil direction has degree at least half the
main degree, up to the exact additive constant. At the prize maximum this
is `v>=2^36-2`.

Future symbolic or analytic work should target only this high-degree interval;
constant and low-degree translation pencils are proved impossible. No Modal
computation was used.

If the centered quadratic coefficient `e_2` vanishes, the target interval
starts still higher, at `ceil((2r-4)/3)`; on the official row this is
`(2^38-4)/3`.

# Audit

The contact order is `2(r-v)`, not `r-v`. Centering the four pencil
parameters removes the cubic term, so the first possible correction to
`U^4` is `U^2V^2`. This factor of two is load-bearing.

The characteristic bound is also load-bearing. From `H'=0` one may conclude
that `H` is constant only because the official characteristic exceeds
`deg H<=d`. The maximal-row field-degree theorem supplies that inequality in
both the prime and quadratic-extension branches.

The theorem survives the known boundary positives. The audit verifier
reconstructs the exact `d=8` pencil over `F_97`, checks `(APD0)`, and checks
both the reverse contact and derivative factorization. There `r=1`, the
degree-drop direction is constant (`v=0`), and `(APD2)` is vacuous. At
`d=16`, `r=3`, the lower bound is only `v>=0`. The new restriction begins at
`d>=32` and is official-scale strong.

The complete `d=8` positive census also has no centered `e_2=0` member: all
`192` positives lie in the generic stratum. This does not assert that the
intermediate stratum is empty at larger order.

No numerical census is consumed by the proof.

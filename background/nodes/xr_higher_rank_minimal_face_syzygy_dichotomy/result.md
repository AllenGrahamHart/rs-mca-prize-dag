# Result

The smallest possible rank-two support is no longer an unnamed primitive
trade. At every selector rank it is either the exact local Plucker
face-syzygy space, of dimension `t-3`, or a common `k+2` interpolation core
for the two received directions. Partial denominator collapse is impossible.

After quotienting the regular face relations, the uniform rank-two frontier
starts at active union `a+3`, except for the explicit near-tangent branch of
defect `h-2`. This recovers the existing six-face quotient when `a=4` and
extends its usable conclusion to the open prize selector ranks. The defect is
`3,3,1` on the three RowC rows and `2^33-1,2^33-1,2^32-1` on the three prize
rows, so this theorem classifies but does not pay that branch on any official
P-A row.

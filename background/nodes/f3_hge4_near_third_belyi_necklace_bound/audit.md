# Audit

1. The center is `S=(U+V)/2`. Using `U` instead loses the cancellation of the
   constant shift under the Euler operator.
2. The hypothesis `e<h` is load-bearing twice: `c=h+e<2h` removes quadratic
   endpoint corrections and makes the degree-drop Mason argument work.
3. The characteristic bound is stronger than `p>m` because coefficient
   uniqueness through degree `c-1` uses the scalars `3h+j` up to
   `4h+e-1`.
4. The factor two in `(NTB4)` is for ordered sides. It is not divided away
   when a generic pair and its swap are distinct scaling orbits.
5. A geometric source scaling relating two cyclotomic pairs lies in `mu_m`;
   otherwise the geometric-cover count would undercount arithmetic orbits.
6. `(NTB6)` is an allowance debit, not an assertion that those orbit counts
   vanish. Every level ledger must add all analytic debits before comparing
   the unresolved remainder with `(21/2)m^2`.
7. The necklace count grows too quickly to pay every near-third cell. No
   extrapolation beyond the printed five additional cells is consumed.
8. The downstream dual-gap theorem may prove a cell empty even when its
   necklace count is over budget. That stronger zero theorem supersedes the
   debit; it does not alter `(NTB4)`.

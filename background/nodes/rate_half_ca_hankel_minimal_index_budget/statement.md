# Two-sided minimal-index budget for moving Hankel kernels

- **status:** PROVED
- **closure:** proof
- **consumer:** `rate_half_band_closure`

Let `C=RS[F,D,k]`, put `N=|D|` and `R=N-k`, and let

```text
M(Z)=M_r(y_0)+Z M_r(y_1)
```

be the `(R-r) x (r+1)` syndrome Hankel pencil from
`rate_half_ca_hankel_split_pencil_equivalence`. Suppose its generic rank is
`1<=rho<=r`, and put

```text
A=R+1-2rho.
```

The generic right and left kernels are generated by shifts of one homogeneous
degree-`rho` apolar form `Q_Z`.  After clearing denominators and making its
coefficient vector primitive over `F[Z]`, let `e` be its coefficient degree in
`Z`.  If `e>=1` and a generic-rank supported slope exists, the fixed
evaluation-domain factors are necessarily simple; let their number be `s`
and put `d=rho-s`.  Then

```text
(A+s)e <= d,                                             (MI1)
```

and the number `T` of finite slopes carrying a domain-split degree-`r`
locator satisfies

```text
T <= rho-Ae + floor((N-s)e/d).                           (MI2)
```

If `e=0`, the generic kernel is fixed and the proved fixed-kernel theorem gives
`T<=rho` for a column-far pair.  A boundary apolar generator whose affine
degree is below `rho` carries no split locator at a generic-rank slope and is
also covered by the exceptional term.

The omitted rank-zero case is vacuous for a column-far pair: the whole pencil
is zero, so both endpoint syndromes vanish and every split locator is common.

The argument is characteristic-free: it uses the divided-power apolar action,
for which the syndrome Hankel matrix is the literal catalecticant, and the
Kronecker canonical form of a matrix pencil.  It does not import the
characteristic hypothesis or the missing verifier cited by
`hankel_rank_profile_entropy`.

## Official rate-half corollary

For

```text
N=2^41,       R=2^40,       r<=R/2-2,
```

every column-far syndrome pencil has at most `r+1` supported finite slopes.
Indeed, the full-column-rank branch is already proved.  In every deficient
branch `A` is an odd integer at least `5`; `(MI1)` and `(MI2)` give

```text
T<=rho                    when A>=7,
T<=rho+1                  when A=5.
```

Consequently the first-transition candidate

```text
a_RH(q)=N-floor(q/2^128)+1
```

is the exact adjacent MCA agreement whenever

```text
floor(q/2^128)<=2^39-1,
```

including all admissible `q<2^167`.  In the CA-only block, only the final
strict budget `2^39` (`r=R/2-1`, possible `A=3`) and the half-distance budget
`2^39+1` (`r=R/2`, possible `A=1` or `3`) remain.

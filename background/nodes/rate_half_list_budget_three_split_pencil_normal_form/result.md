# Result

The B*=3 predecessor is no longer posed first as a rank question for a matrix
with `6d` columns. Every singular witness must supply four disjoint subgroup
block locators satisfying four split-pencil syzygies with constant, linear,
or at most three explicitly located quadratic edge factors.

The remaining theorem can target this constant-degree pencil directly:
classify such factorizations of the multiplicative-coset vanishing polynomial,
or construct one and recover the resulting four-codeword list. This aligns
the list leaf with the project's existing base-field-normalized split-pencil
census while preserving the exact B*=3 endpoint.

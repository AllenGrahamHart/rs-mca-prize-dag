# Audit - L1 quotient-chart bipolar entropy closure

## Checked axes

1. Ties at `ell/2` use one fixed orientation, preserving injectivity.
2. Petal and core exception sets are separate; their exponents add.
3. Dense core fibers are encoded by orientation bits, so an almost-full fiber
   is charged by its holes rather than by its occupied size.
4. `2^M` and `2^N` combine before applying the cutoff.
5. Strict-window and thin-edge CRT bounds cover the entire degree strip.
6. Background agreements are reconstructed from the numerator and receive no
   independent support multiplier.
7. Per-chart closure does not imply aggregate chart control.

## Remaining attack

Prove that large petal/core bipolar entropy is paid by a root-pinning,
split-in-subspace, or natural-scale profile theorem; separately control
first-match source-chart multiplicity and arbitrary-locator charts.

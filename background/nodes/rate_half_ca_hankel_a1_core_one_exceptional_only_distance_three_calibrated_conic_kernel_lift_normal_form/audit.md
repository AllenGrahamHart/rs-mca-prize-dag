# Audit

1. The row scale includes `B(x)`: omitting it leaves the residues proportional
   to `D_i(x)/B(x)`, not a quadratic conic.
2. The quotient `J_x` has degree at most `e`, not less than `e`; its leading
   coefficient is the nonzero scale `s_x`.
3. The exponent in `(CKL6)` is row count minus replication,
   `(6e+3)-(2e+1)=4e+2`.
4. The theorem does not infer a low-dimensional family of kernel lifts from
   the three-dimensional residue image. That inference is exactly what the
   earlier saturated cyclic route fence warns against.

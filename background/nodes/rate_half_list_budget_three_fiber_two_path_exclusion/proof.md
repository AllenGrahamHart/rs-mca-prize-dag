# Proof

Condition `(F2P1)` gives a polynomial `K_i` of degree `s-1` with

```text
H_i(Y)=(Y-r_i^2)K_i(Y).
```

Substituting `Y=X^2` and cancelling the deleted root gives

```text
A_i(X)=(X+r_i)K_i(X^2).                               (1)
```

Assume `(F2P3)` and put `G_i=lambda_iK_i`. Equation `(1)` becomes

```text
X(G_0+G_1+G_2)(X^2)
 +(r_0G_0+r_1G_1+r_2G_2)(X^2)=0.                     (2)
```

The first summand in `(2)` is odd in `X` and the second is even. Equivalently,
their monomial supports have opposite parity, in every characteristic. Hence

```text
G_0+G_1+G_2=0,
r_0G_0+r_1G_1+r_2G_2=0.                              (3)
```

The two-by-three constant matrix in `(3)` has rank two because the `r_i` are
distinct. Its kernel is generated by

```text
(r_1-r_2,r_2-r_0,r_0-r_1).                           (4)
```

Applying `(4)` coefficient by coefficient, or taking the kernel over
`F[Y]`, gives one `K in F[Y]` such that

```text
(G_0,G_1,G_2)=K(r_1-r_2,r_2-r_0,r_0-r_1).           (5)
```

All scalars in `(5)` are nonzero. Since every `lambda_i` is nonzero, each
`K_i` is a nonzero scalar multiple of the same `K`. The common-degree
hypothesis gives

```text
deg K=deg K_i=s-1>=1.                                 (6)
```

By `(1)`, the nonconstant polynomial `K(X^2)` divides every `A_i`, contrary
to pairwise coprimality. This proves the abstract exclusion.

For a direct common-`X^2` completion in a path-plus-singleton chamber, each
tight-triangle block has one deleted root, so its completed locator is
`H_i(X^2)` and its actual locator is `(F2P2)`. The four omitted-triple blocks
are disjoint, hence the three locators in the printed relation are pairwise
coprime. Their relation is nondegenerate by the linear Grassmann atlas.
Finally `d=2s` and official `d=2^39` give `s=2^38>=2`. The abstract theorem
therefore excludes both path chambers at fiber size two. The preceding
Vandermonde theorem excludes every `m>=3`. QED.

# Audit

1. Non-fullness is load-bearing. For `m=2h`, the pair
   `P=X^h-1`, `Q=X^h+1` has constant difference and fills `mu_m`; here the
   complement degree is zero and the conclusion fails.
2. The characteristic condition is used twice: `X^m-1` must be separable,
   and the leading coefficient `dh/m` of `H` must be nonzero. The official
   prime-field rows have `p>n>=m`, so both uses are automatic.
3. The generic conclusion is `3h<=m`, not strict. At `m=3,h=1`, any two
   distinct roots give linear locators differing by a constant and leave one
   complement root. Strictness comes only from dyadic `m`.
4. The proof uses equality of every nonconstant locator coefficient through
   `Q-P=d`; agreement of only some moments is insufficient.
5. Primitivity is not used in the algebra. It is retained only in the
   counted currency `E_h^prim`, so the deletion also applies before the
   primitive filter.
6. The result changes a generator boundary, not the HGE4 status. No estimate
   for the retained widths is inferred from their surviving the gate.

# Audit

1. The square conclusion uses `2N | q-1`, not merely `N | q-1`.
2. Terminal trace and product are both required to infer `c^N=d^N=1`.
3. Distinctness is not implied by torsion and remains a separate nonzero gate.
4. In `(TFR5)`, Frobenius acts on coefficients; the variable `z` is fixed.
5. The factor `E_4^(-1)` in the reciprocal identity is essential.
6. Reciprocal Frobenius inverts `t,c,d` separately. It is not the orientation
   involution, which divides the complementary roots by `t`.
7. A valid packet need not descend to `F_p` in the unitary chamber.

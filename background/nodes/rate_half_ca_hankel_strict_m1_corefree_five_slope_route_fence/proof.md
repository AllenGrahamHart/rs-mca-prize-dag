# Proof

All arithmetic is in `F_17`. The coefficient vectors below are in ascending
`X`-degree.

## 1. Explicit pencil

Let

```text
q_0=(7,0,9,1),       q_1=(0,12,4,0).
```

Then `Q(z)=q_0+zq_1` is `(M1F1)`. Direct multiplication gives the five
factorizations in `(M1F3)`. Their root sets are disjoint and omit only `14`,
so their common intersection is empty. Equivalently, `q_0` and `q_1` have no
common evaluation-domain root; this is `s=0`.

Let `M_i` be the `5 x 4` Hankel matrix of `y_i` from `(M1F2)`. Direct
convolution gives

```text
M_0 q_0=0,
M_0 q_1+M_1 q_0=0,
M_1 q_1=0.                                            (1)
```

Thus `M(z)Q(z)=0` identically. Row reduction at every `z in F_17` and at
infinity gives rank three. The kernel is consequently one-dimensional at
every projective slope and is generated by `Q(z)` (by `q_1` at infinity).
The infinity generator has affine degree two and a boundary root, so it is
not a degree-three locator split over `D`.

The finite split fibers of this one-dimensional kernel are exactly the five
in `(M1F3)`. The endpoint kernels `<q_0>` and `<q_1>` are distinct, so no
degree-three split locator is common to both syndrome endpoints. The
split-pencil equivalence therefore supplies a column-far received pair with
exactly these five CA-bad slopes. This proves `(M1F4)`.

## 2. Complete locator-line census

Enumerate the `560` three-subsets of `D` and map each to the coefficient
vector of its monic cubic locator. For every pair of distinct vectors,
enumerate the `17` affine points on their coefficient line and retain those
that are split locators. Deduplicating lines by their retained locator set
gives

```text
number of split locators on line: 3      4    5     6    7   14
number of distinct rich lines:     12192  960  1360  2800 480  120.
```

Intersecting the root sets along each line refines the nontrivial rows to

```text
(line size, fixed-core size):
(5,0):16, (5,1):1344, (6,1):2800, (7,1):480, (14,2):120.
```

All core-free lines of sizes three and four are also counted; none has size
above five. Hence the core-free maximum is five and exactly sixteen lines
attain it.

For each of those sixteen lines, write its coefficient pencil as
`q_0+zq_1` and solve the fifteen linear equations `(1)` in the sixteen
moment variables `(y_0,y_1)`. Exact row reduction gives nullity one. For its
unique projective solution, exact row reduction of all seventeen finite
Hankel specializations and the infinity endpoint gives rank three every
time. This proves the classification and compatibility assertions. The
registered verifier performs this complete census and replays the printed
witness. QED.

# Result

The singular DSP8 contribution is now bounded by the exact
same-triple-corrected cross-orbit trace energy

```text
E_tr=sum_c N_c(N_c-6),
```

rather than the crude square of the total nodal point count. In the
three-cubic-root case this energy is exactly

```text
E_tr=(N^2+2|S|^2)/3-6N,
```

where `S` is one cubic-character bias of the nodal parameter set. A bias at
most `4N/5` places the nodal contribution below `1812n^2`, leaving more than
`103n^2` of the live primitive allowance. No bias estimate is needed when
`N<=5.9n^(2/3)`: that distribution-free range already fits the nodal budget.
The proved point cap is below `6.6332n^(2/3)`, so only the narrow interval
between those two coefficients needs trace balance or a target-sensitive
replacement. The smooth-trace payment remains open.

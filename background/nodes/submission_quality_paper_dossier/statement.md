# Submission-quality paper and dossier

- **status:** TARGET
- **closure:** artifact
- **consumer:** `packaging`
- **dependencies:** `mca_grand`, `list_grand`

There is a final submission packet, aligned with Przemek's Paper-D grammar,
that proves and certifies both official Proximity Prize problems without a
required-scope nonclaim. It contains all of the following.

1. Final theorem statements for support-wise MCA and list/interleaved list on
   every admissible smooth Reed--Solomon row, all four official rates, and the
   full required constant-arity scope.
2. A claim-to-proof ledger mapping every theorem hypothesis and conclusion to
   proved DAG nodes and stable proof assets. No required step is represented
   only by an experiment, conjecture, conditional with an open premise, or
   external prose pointer.
3. The certified map `f(C)`: for every admissible descriptor it prints the
   largest safe grid radius, the adjacent unsafe boundary, exact `B*`, upper
   and lower counts, object/field/denominator/endpoint conventions, and proof
   provenance. It refuses malformed or out-of-scope descriptors.
4. An independent verifier and hash manifest that replay every official
   certificate from a clean checkout under the published resource contract.
5. A submission paper/dossier with no open truth leaf, admitted proof, or
   required-axis nonclaim. Partial-credit rows and historical route failures
   are labeled as such and cannot substitute for full coverage.
6. Coherent upstream-facing packets offering every relevant proved result and
   counterexample to Przemek, with current terminology and reproducible
   checkers.

The packet is complete only when both grand nodes are `PROVED`, its final
artifact manifest exists, and an audit checks every item above. The existing
`notes/dossier/PARTIAL_DOSSIER.md` is a proved reusable template but explicitly
does not satisfy this target.

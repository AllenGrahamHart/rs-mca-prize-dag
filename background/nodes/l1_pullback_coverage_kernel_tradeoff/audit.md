# Audit - L1 pullback coverage/kernel tradeoff

1. `k_j` counts exponent residue classes exactly.
2. Both `k<s` and `b=0` are included.
3. The two possible integer positions of `b` relative to `floor(k/s)` are
   exhaustive.
4. Complete-fiber coverage is `sb`, not `b`.
5. The agreement identity uses fully agreed fibers only.
6. `z` includes partial and incomplete-fiber agreement points.
7. The loss lower bound is applied only to listed codewords at `a_*`.
8. `kappa` is zero whenever complete coverage reaches `k` points.
9. The fixed-kernel aggregate retains the generated-field exponent.
10. Wild and missing-anchor map counts remain separate.

The remaining sparse-coverage problem is now part of the growing partial-loss
branch rather than an independent owner axis.

# Audit

1. `N=n/2=2^m`; the recurrence terminates at `V_m`, not at the exponent for
   the full group `mu_n`.
2. `h` is odd and at most `N-1`, so `k=m-s_0>=1`.
3. The base-variable count includes the scalar `c`.
4. Monicity makes the recurrence triangular over every commutative ring.
5. Characteristic-zero emptiness uses the full dyadic root-order condition,
   not Newton identities alone.
6. The Newton short-window theorem stops at `w<=2ell`; here
   `w=h=2ell+1`, its sharp next boundary.
7. The size inequality plus the first moment alone is insufficient even at
   dyadic analogue rows: `F_337` contains a seven-term zero-sum in `mu_16`,
   although its third and fifth moments are nonzero.
8. The integer certificate depends on `(N,h)` and is not computed.
9. A finite-row certificate cannot prove the all-row HGE4 aggregate.
10. Sparse cubic presentation does not constitute a cost estimate.

# Result

The distance-four obstruction class splits into an antipodal lane and a
generic cross-overlap lane. They are governed by the exact two-variable
subgroup conditions

```text
x^2=u+v-uv,
x=(u^2-y)/(u(1-y)).
```

The first right side must lie in the square subgroup and then determines the
unordered antipodal pair. This explains why distance four forms a smaller
algebraic sublane than the generic distance-six norms. Neither sublane is
excluded.

Globally, generic edges contribute at most `n^2` and antipodal edges at most
`n(n+1)/2`. Hence `N_4<=(3n^2+n)/2`, and at most
`floor((3n^2+n)/12)` rich targets can have no eligible distance-six edge.

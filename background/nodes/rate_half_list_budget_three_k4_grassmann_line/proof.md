# Proof

Write

```text
B(X)=B_0+X B_1.
```

The Plucker identity gives `B(X) wedge B(X)=0`. Since the characteristic is
odd, comparison of the constant, linear, and quadratic coefficients yields

```text
B_0 wedge B_0=0,
B_0 wedge B_1=0,
B_1 wedge B_1=0.                                      (1)
```

Both `B_0` and `B_1` are nonzero. Indeed, every `b_ij` has nonzero leading
coefficient, while distinct roots prevent all six constant coefficients from
vanishing. They are linearly independent, since proportionality would make
all six linear polynomials scalar multiples of one polynomial and hence give
them one common root.

A nonzero two-vector in dimension four is decomposable exactly when its
self-wedge vanishes. Thus `B_0` and `B_1` represent two distinct two-planes.
Their mixed wedge vanishes exactly when those planes meet nontrivially. Their
intersection is one-dimensional, because equality would make the two-vectors
proportional. Choose a nonzero vector `w` on that common line and vectors
`u,v` so that

```text
B_0=w wedge u,       B_1=w wedge v.
```

The vectors `w,u,v` are independent, and `(KGL2)` follows.

The Plucker gate proves that the block-locator vector `A(X)` belongs over
`F(X)` to the two-plane represented by `B(X)`. Equation `(KGL2)` identifies
that plane as `<w,u+Xv>`, proving `(KGL3)`. The fixed space `<w,u,v>` has
dimension three, so it has a one-dimensional annihilator generated by some
nonzero `lambda`. Applying it to `(KGL3)` proves `(KGL4)`.

It remains to show that no coordinate `lambda_i` vanishes. Suppose
`lambda_i=0`. For the other three indices `j`, the three vectors

```text
p_j=(w_j,u_j,v_j) in F^3
```

are linearly dependent. Hence there are constants `a,b,c`, not all zero,
such that

```text
a w_j+b u_j+c v_j=0                                  (2)
```

for all three indices.

If `c!=0`, substituting
`v_j=-(a/c)w_j-(b/c)u_j` into `(KGL2)` gives, for each of the three pairs,

```text
b_jk(X)=(w_j u_k-w_k u_j)(1-(b/c)X).                 (3)
```

The exact degree-one hypothesis makes both factors needed in `(3)` nonzero,
so all three edge polynomials have the same root `c/b`, contradicting the
disjoint edge blocks. If `c=0`, equation `(2)` makes the constant coefficient
`w_j u_k-w_k u_j` vanish for all three pairs; their nonzero linear
polynomials then all have root zero, the same contradiction. Thus every
`lambda_i` is nonzero.

No proper subsum in `(KGL4)` can vanish. A one-term sum is nonzero. A
two-term sum would make two monic `A_i` proportional and hence equal,
contradicting their disjoint nonempty root blocks. A three-term sum, compared
with `(KGL4)`, would force the remaining nonzero term to vanish.

Finally the `K_4` incidence row has two full points, all six singleton edge
blocks, no singleton coordinate, and four blocks of size `d-2`. The partition
identity from the split-pencil normal form is therefore exactly `(KGL5)`,
with `deg E=2+6=8`. QED.

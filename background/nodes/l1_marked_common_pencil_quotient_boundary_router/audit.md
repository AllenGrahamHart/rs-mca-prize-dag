# Audit - L1 marked common-pencil quotient-boundary router

## Checked axes

1. The core, not merely the selected petals, must be partitioned by the same
   quotient polynomial `P`.
2. `B_D` removes every complete defect fiber; otherwise `(QB3)` is not
   canonical.
3. The quotient factor describes the defect locator, not the receiver or the
   full agreement set.
4. The factor `q^(2p)` comes from the exact marked CRT theorem; it is not
   replaced by an unproved singleton assertion at the lower endpoint.
5. Polynomiality requires both boundary and polarity bounds to be fixed.
6. The theorem is per source chart and does not silently solve first-match
   overlap.

## Remaining attack

Bound or profile-own the actual quotient-core counts `Q(B)` with a
source-coupled theorem, or prove that every unowned common-pencil family has
bounded first-match multiplicity and unbounded partial-fiber boundary that is
paid by another structural column.

# Audit

1. No sixth-root normalization is used; it would split into two square
   classes and hide a case.
2. The even/odd decomposition is `F=E(X^2)-XB(X^2)`.
3. Because the degree is even, the norm identity has no leading minus sign:
   `F(X)F(-X)=G(X^2)`.
4. Reduced support is needed so squaring does not merge antipodal roots.
5. In the converse, a zero of `B` would also be a zero of `E` and hence a
   repeated root of `G`; separability excludes it.
6. The unit-ideal theorem does not classify a finite characteristic before a
   checked `Delta_6` is supplied.

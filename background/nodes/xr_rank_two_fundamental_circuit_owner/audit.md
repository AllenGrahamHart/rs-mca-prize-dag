# Audit

1. The owner is relative to a declared total order and the lexicographically
   first coefficient basis, so it is deterministic rather than existential.
2. Uniqueness uses independence of the anchor basis, not uniqueness of a
   Maxwell core or of a trade containing the same block.
3. Every fundamental relation is support-minimal before the arity atlas is
   invoked; the four/five conclusion is therefore licensed.
4. The identity-coordinate argument proves that the owner vectors span the
   entire scaling kernel, not only the all-one trade vector.
5. The `q=3` Mobius conclusion uses both distinct slopes and pairwise
   nonproportional row classes from the split-pencil dependency.
6. The theorem leaves at most four anchors per trade, not four anchors
   globally. It supplies no cross-core count by itself.
7. No Modal or candidate-row numerical evidence is used.

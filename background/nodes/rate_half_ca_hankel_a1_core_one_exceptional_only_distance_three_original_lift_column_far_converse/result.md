# Result

Every contracted distance-three design lifts to a two-parameter family of
original rate-half syndrome pairs, and every lift is column-far. A common
full locator would force all external residual locators into one fixed set of
at most `r+1` points, contradicting their exact `6e+3`-point union.

The official distance-three branch is therefore reduced without qualification
to one algebraic question: does the pair-Lagrange generator admit the exact
external split design? Existence gives a genuine counterexample; nonexistence
closes the branch.

# Audit

- The antipodal derivative product contributes `-4u`, while the triple
  product contributes a second minus sign. Both are required for `(DOR2)`.
- In the constant-product branch, `A=X^eE(X+c/X)` is a polynomial identity;
  differentiating only after setting `E(u)=0` gives the factor `c^(e-1)`.
- The factor `U^2-4c` is nonzero at every orbit root because exceptional
  pairs have two distinct members.
- `(DOR4)` uses that `E` is split and squarefree over the base field. It is
  not asserted for an abstract nonsplit orbit polynomial.
- The verifier compares direct products over all active outside rows with
  the reduced formulas for both branches over `F_97`, and also checks the
  global product reduction.

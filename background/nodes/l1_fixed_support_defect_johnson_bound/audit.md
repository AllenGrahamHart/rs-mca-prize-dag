2. Exact-defect saturation makes the cross determinant nonzero for distinct
   pairs.
3. The common defect locator is coprime to the support locator because core
   and petals are disjoint.
4. The Johnson inequality is used only with a strictly positive denominator.
5. The zero-denominator `F_7` fixture is populated by all six two-subsets of
   a four-point core.
6. The sharp positive fixture attains the bound with three defect sets.
7. The aggregate adds neither a field-size factor nor a syndrome factor.
8. Threshold arithmetic retains the background gap `g=ell-b`; silently
   setting `b=0` would overstate the paid region.
9. The payment is not the global B11 Johnson gate: a fixed support may have a
   positive local denominator while its total agreement remains globally
   sub-Johnson.
10. Non-intrinsic chart multiplicity remains outside the theorem.

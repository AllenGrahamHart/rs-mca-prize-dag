# Audit

1. Couplings are normalized by one `pi`, while product differences are
   normalized by `pi^2`; hence `(SQC3)` uses `pi C alpha_k`, equivalently
   `pi^2 c_u alpha_k`, not `C alpha_k`.
2. Center-independence is exact because all rebased product generators are
   integral differences of the original generators.
3. A zero coupling does not make the packet ideal zero: a star has a leaf,
   and shifted-product Sidonicity makes its normalized difference nonzero.
4. The quotient lift remains packet metadata and is used in the row-prime
   containment even when its characteristic-zero coupling is zero.
5. Cutoff `c=35`, not `34` or `36`, detects `P>=36` through
   `(P-c)_+>0`.
6. `(SQC5)` is restricted to official primes `p=1 mod n`, exactly the scope
   of the quotient-algebra reduction.
7. Enlarging a derivative ideal makes its positive generator divide the old
   one; the direction is `s_35|s_18`.
8. Scalar support does not contain the `Dbar_17` value. Every retained row
   still needs an exact class-sensitive moment evaluation.

# Result

The exact external incidence design is self-dual at the polynomial-product
level: multiplying the degree-`e` row polynomials gives the external-slope
polynomial to the exact power `r=2e+1`. Comparing the exceptional and
internal specializations forces one support-only `r`th-power residue per
matched exceptional pair.

At official scale the old root-unit exponent and new power-residue exponent
factor over disjoint prime sets. A distance-three classifier should apply
both support-only gate families before allocating any internal slope, fiber
scalar, or block variable.

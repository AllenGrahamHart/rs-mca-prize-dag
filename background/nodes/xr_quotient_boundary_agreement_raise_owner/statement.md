# XR quotient boundaries are owned by the agreement raise

- **status:** PROVED
- **closure:** proof
- **consumer:** `xr_tangent_support_mismatch_bridge`
- **dependencies:** `xr_quotient_image_remainder_one_boundary_descent`,
  `xr_mismatch_terminal_tangent_agreement_raise`

Let `C=RS[F,D,k]`, fix `A>=k+1`, and let `C_fib` be any finite family of
declared quotient fiber sizes. Retain the notation `Q_c(>=A)`, `Q_c(A)`, and
`T_c(A)` from the remainder-one boundary descent, and write

```text
B_a(u,v)={z in F:there is p in C with agr(u+zv,p)>=a}.
```

Then

```text
union_(c in C_fib) Q_c(>=A)
  subseteq union_(c in C_fib) Q_c(A) union B_(A+1)(u,v).   (QAO1)
```

Indeed, every slope in `T_c(A)` is explained by `p_z=c_0+z c_1` on a
pure-fiber core of size at least `A` and one additional discrepancy
coordinate. It therefore belongs to the original received line's bad-slope
set at agreement `A+1`, independently of the core and fiber scale.

Consequently, quotient migration contributes no separate one-point-tail
boundary currency. A threshold induction need only pay the exact-threshold
quotient image and carry every boundary factor wholesale to its existing
`B_(A+1)` term.

This theorem does not bound the union of exact-`A` quotient images across
thresholds, pay `B_(A+1)`, aggregate generic charts, or close XR.

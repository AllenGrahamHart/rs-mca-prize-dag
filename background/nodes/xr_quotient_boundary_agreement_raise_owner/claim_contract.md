# Claim contract

- **claim:** across every declared quotient scale, the full threshold image
  is contained in the exact-`A` quotient image together with the original
  bad-slope set at agreement `A+1`.
- **scope:** any RS code, received affine line, finite declared fiber family,
  and `A>=k+1` covered by the remainder-one boundary theorem.
- **currency:** distinct quotient-witnessed finite slopes, deduplicated across
  supports, pure-fiber cores, and scales.
- **consumer effect:** removes the one-point-tail boundary as an independent
  XR debit; it is carried by the existing agreement-raise recurrence.
- **nonclaims:** no bound on exact-threshold quotient images, `B_(A+1)`, or
  generic-chart slopes, and no mismatch-bridge close.
- **falsifier:** a boundary slope whose tangent codeword fails to agree on the
  pure-fiber core plus its added point, or a slope in some `Q_c(>=A)` lying
  outside both `Q_c(A)` and `B_(A+1)(u,v)`.

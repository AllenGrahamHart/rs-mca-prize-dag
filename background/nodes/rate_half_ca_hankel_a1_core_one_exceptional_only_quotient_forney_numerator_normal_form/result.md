# Result

Every quotient-distance branch now has one exact algebraic normal form. The
support is encoded by its monic locator `B_T`; all support weights are
reconstructed from a unique coprime numerator `F` of degree `h-3`, whose
leading coefficient is the nonzero crossing scalar `Theta_2`. Exact
saturation makes `F` root-free on the complete relation support
`R_A union T`, not only on `T`.

This is the general-distance version of the distance-three barycentric
router. It uses no enumeration or Modal resource. The remaining task is to
combine the `F/B_T` key equation with the split-locator incidence system to
exclude or classify the surviving high-distance supports.

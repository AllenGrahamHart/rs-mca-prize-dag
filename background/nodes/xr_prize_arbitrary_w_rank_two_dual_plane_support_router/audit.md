# Audit

- The dual plane is formed after extending rows by zero to the union of their
  supports; every row still annihilates `W`.
- `X` has no base point of `[F:G]` by its definition as the support union.
- Four-block coefficient directions are distinct. Five-block directions may
  repeat twice, and that case is retained and routed.
- `(DP3)` needs two distinct slopes, not a standalone three-row rank-one
  subtrade.
- `(DP4)` is the arbitrary-`W` locator space. It does not impose the smaller
  dual-GRS degree bound valid only at `u=0`.
- The converse reconstructs a trade, not a selected-error realization; the
  blocks must still be certified agreement blocks in the parent compiler.
- No Modal or large local computation is used.

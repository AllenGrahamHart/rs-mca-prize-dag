# Audit

1. The resultant is a polynomial in the auxiliary variable `X`; taking one
   numerical specialization would lose the polynomial-identity criterion.
2. All thirty-six Hasse derivatives through index `35` are retained. The
   first three give only the proved candidate superset.
3. Roots of `q_O` are counted with multiplicity. Repeated quotient values
   modulo a bad prime do not invalidate the product-zero argument.
4. Exactness is for odd prime support. No equality of valuations, contents,
   annihilators, or Fitting orders is asserted.
5. One block resultant can have `X`-degree `143,360`; the theorem gives no
   coefficient, memory, runtime, factorization, or dollar bound.
6. A complete campaign must process every block and completely factor every
   odd content. Partial blocks or factors are evidence only.

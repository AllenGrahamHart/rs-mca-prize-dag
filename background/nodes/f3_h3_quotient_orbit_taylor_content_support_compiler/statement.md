# H3 quotient-orbit Taylor-content support compiler

- **status:** PROVED

- **consumers:** `f3_h3_dsp8_correlation_bound`,

For a canonical quotient-orbit polynomial `q_O(T)` over `R=Z[1/2]`, define
the cutoff-35 Taylor packet and its one orbit resultant by

```text
F_35(T,X)=sum_(i=0)^35 Pcal_n^[i](T)X^i
          =Pcal_n(T+X) mod X^36,                         (TCS1)

C_O,35(X)=Res_T(q_O(T),F_35(T,X)) in R[X].              (TCS2)
```

The polynomial `C_O,35` is nonzero. Let `c_O,35` be the positive odd
generator of its coefficient-content ideal in `R`:

```text
(c_O,35)=(all coefficients of C_O,35) subset R.         (TCS3)
```

If `s_O,35` is the exact orbitwise scalar annihilator, then

```text
rad(c_O,35)=rad(s_O,35)                                  (TCS4)
```

as odd integer radicals. Equivalently, for every odd prime `p`,

```text
p divides c_O,35
 iff q_O and Pcal_n^[0],...,Pcal_n^[35]
     have a common root over algebraic closure(F_p)
 iff p divides s_O,35.                                  (TCS5)
```

Thus CR-001's exact cutoff-35 support can be generated by one
Taylor-resultant content per canonical block, without a Smith form and
without the false positives of the three-resultant gcd screen. At `n=8192`
there are `24,534` independent resultants, each with

```text
deg_T(q_O)<=4,096,       deg_X(C_O,35)<=143,360.         (TCS6)
```

The total possible `X`-degree across all blocks remains `2,347,950,150`.
This theorem does not identify valuations, compute any official resultant or
content, bound coefficient growth, prove a runtime/memory/cost bound, factor
any `c_O,35`, estimate the retained moment, or close C36'.

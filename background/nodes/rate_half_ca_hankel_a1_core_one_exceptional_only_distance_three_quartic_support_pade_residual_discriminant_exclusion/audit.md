# Audit

1. Tail norms need not be squares. The discriminant argument uses only the
   at least `e-t` good involution pairs.
2. The scalar relating `K_u` to `P_HR_u` depends on `u` but cancels between
   two internal indices.
3. Equality on aligned coordinates becomes a polynomial identity because
   both sides have degree at most four and there are more than four points.
4. Distinct good norms are coprime squares because disjoint involution pairs
   have distinct quotient coordinates.
5. The discriminant has `Z`-degree at most `2k<=8`; the official good-index
   count is overwhelmingly larger.
6. Squarefreeness is essential in the valuation parity argument. It is
   supplied by the complement being a divisor of squarefree `P_Z`.
7. `R_2` is nonzero because every good norm has nonzero quadratic leading
   coefficient.
8. Projective distinctness is reproved from three good calibrated indices;
   it is not imported from the zero-tail theorem without checking tails.

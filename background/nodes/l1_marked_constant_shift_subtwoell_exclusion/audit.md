# Audit - L1 marked constant-shift sub-two-ell exclusion

## Checked axes

1. Multiplication uses the product of every selected missing-set locator.
2. The enlarged pair is not called saturated: its gcd is exactly `J`.
3. The rank-three common divisor has degree `d+v-ell`, strictly larger than
   `deg J=v` precisely because `d>ell`.
4. The two-block decomposition needs the strict upper bound `d+v<2ell`.
5. Three distinct pencil labels are required to force one common value.
6. The L1 application requires a contributor-dependent common
   constant-shift pencil; arbitrary maximal-source petals need not have one.

## Remaining attack

Treat two dense petals, arbitrary locator triples, or prove a stable
multi-block version at and above `d+v=2ell`.

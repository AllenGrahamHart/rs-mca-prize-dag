# Proof

The proved `xr_target_budget_audit` packet records QA.22 at six clean-rate
candidates. Its source compiler is explicit: the live polynomial object is
`R_post(u,v;A)<=16n^3` for every pair `(u,v)`, after the MCA quotient/tangent
strip. The exact QA.22 arithmetic adds its quotient/dihedral staircase and
tangent columns to that per-pair reserve.

The former binding W3 statement instead fixed a received word `U` and asked for its
actual non-planted list-codeword count. Its units are codewords per received
word, not slopes per ordered pair. Equality of the integer threshold `B*` does
not identify those sets or their multiplicities.

The proved `xr_sameslope_list_crossover` observation supplies a structural
dictionary inside one slope: distinct aligned supports yield distinct
codewords and are far-spread. It does not bound how many ordered pairs or
slopes can represent one W3 word, nor sum the per-pair bounds in W3's
quantifier order. The XR packet itself labels same-slope population as the
list lane's debt. Hence it is not a missing implicit conversion theorem.

By `ww_two_class_minimal_ledger_reduction`, W3 had a correct minimal list-side
assembly: `List(U)=p(U)+N_nonplant(U)`. That identity neither needs nor
licenses the MCA `16n^3` reserve. Demoting the QA.22 edge to evidence therefore
removed an unsupported type conversion while retaining every exact arithmetic
fact. The later scope counterexample does not alter this proof; it demonstrates
that the list-side upper inequality fails at the unsafe spending cell, outside
literal safe-side W3.

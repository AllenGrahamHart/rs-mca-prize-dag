# Proof

Fix an admissible tuple `(p,e,s,rho)`. Put `q=p^e`, `n=2^s`, and
`k=rho*n`. These formulas are deterministic exact integer operations. The
official rate set has power-of-two denominators, so `k` is integral on every
admissible row. The descriptor checks `q<2^256`, `k<=2^40`, and `n | q-1`.
The last condition is exactly what guarantees an order-`n` subgroup in the
cyclic group `F_q^*`.

The official MCA denominator is `q`, so its exact integer threshold is

```text
B*=floor(q/2^128).
```

For agreement `a`, the closed Hamming radius is `(n-a)/n`; the adjacent lower
agreement is `a-1`, whose real boundary is `(n-a+1)/n`. These formulas prove
every row-derived scalar emitted by the implementation. The compiler's
theorem-packet counts and convention axes are deliberately not generated by
this map and retain their own proof provenance.

Under the official caps all generated integers have at most 256 bits. Binary
exponentiation, shifts, exact division, hashing, and JSON rendering therefore
take polynomial time in the input and output bit lengths. The map is total on
the admissible family. Primality of `p` and the code/domain provenance are
external preconditions, printed in every output rather than silently claimed.

`verify.py` performs two independent regressions. It reconstructs the pinned
`F_(17^32)` row and obtains `n=512`, `k=256`, and `B*=6`. It also reconstructs
all four official rates on every existing entry of
`critical/nodes/wild_row_audit/wild_row_audit.json`, comparing the field order
to that table rather than to newly generated fixtures. It rejects unsupported
rates, the field and dimension caps, failed subgroup divisibility, hostile
oversized exponents, and boolean type smuggling.

`tools/prize_certificate_compiler.py` consumes the descriptor through its v2
schema. It canonically recomputes the full descriptor from `(p,e,s,rho)`,
requires exact object equality, derives every row scalar from that object, and
rejects injected legacy `q`/`n`/`k`/epsilon fields. The compiler audit includes
descriptor-tamper and free-field mutations, establishing the single-source
integration rather than merely the standalone map.

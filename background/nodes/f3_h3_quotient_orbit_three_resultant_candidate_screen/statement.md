# H3 quotient-orbit three-resultant candidate screen

- **status:** PROVED
- **closure:** proof
- **consumers:** `f3_h3_dsp8_correlation_bound`,
  `f3_h3_official_order_template_survivor`
- **dependencies:** `f3_h3_quotient_orbit_canonical_resultant_manifest`,
  `f3_h3_quotient_algebra_fitting_support_compiler`

Let `q_O(T)` be any canonical quotient-orbit polynomial over
`R=Z[1/2]`, let `Pcal_n^[i]` denote the `i`-th Hasse derivative of the
global shifted-product polynomial, and put

```text
rho_(O,i)=Res_T(q_O(T),Pcal_n^[i](T)) in R,
                                               i=0,1,2.       (TRS1)
```

The three resultants in `(TRS1)` are not all zero. Define the positive odd
integer `g_O` by

```text
(g_O)=(rho_(O,0),rho_(O,1),rho_(O,2)) subset R.    (TRS2)
```

Equivalently, remove signs and powers of two and take the ordinary gcd of
the three resultant numerators, with zero entries allowed.

Let `s_O,35` be the exact orbitwise cutoff-35 scalar annihilator. Then

```text
s_O,35 divides g_O.                                (TRS3)
```

Consequently every odd official prime supporting a target with
`P(t)>=36,R(t)>=1` divides at least one `g_O`. At `n=8192`, a complete
candidate superset is therefore generated by exactly

```text
3*24,534=73,602
```

ordinary univariate resultants, with `Pcal_n^[i]` reduced modulo the
formula-generated `q_O` before each resultant. Complete factorization of all
`g_O`, official-range filtering, and exact row evaluation is a
proof-complete alternative to computing the exact scalar annihilators.

The converse to `(TRS3)` is false in general: the three derivatives may meet
three different roots of `q_O` modulo a prime. Thus this theorem does not
give exact candidate support, bound false-positive inflation or coefficient
size, prove an efficient resultant/factorization algorithm, estimate cost,
bound the retained moment, or close C36'.

# Audit

1. Three Hasse derivatives are used. Two do not suffice for the nonzero
   guarantee when an irreducible block divides a double-root factor of the
   characteristic-zero product polynomial.
2. The resultant gcd is an overcandidate integer. Different derivatives can
   meet different roots of a split orbit polynomial modulo the same prime.
3. Ideal containment has the direction `s_O,35 | g_O`: each resultant is in
   the full derivative ideal, not conversely.
4. Zero resultants are allowed individually. All three cannot vanish because
   product-root multiplicity is at most two.
5. Powers of two are units in `Z[1/2]`; candidate support and the gcd are
   explicitly odd.
6. Complete factorization and exact evaluation of every retained official
   prime are required. A partial factorization or a sampled false-positive
   rate proves no coverage statement.
7. The count `73,602` is a task count, not a runtime, memory, coefficient, or
   dollar bound.

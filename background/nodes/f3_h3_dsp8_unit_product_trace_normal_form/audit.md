# Audit

- The normalization uses the unique cube root in `H`; it would acquire a
  threefold ambiguity if `3` divided the subgroup order.
- The decoration is load-bearing. Product-one normalization alone has a free
  common `H`-scale, but `A(0)=-RS` forces that scale to be `q=rs`.
- `sigma` is a field element, not an independently free subgroup variable.
  It must make both printed quadratics split over `H`.
- The residual nonidentity and six-coordinate conditions are checked after
  forced rescaling. Dropping them enlarges the incidence and loses exactness.
- The upstream ambient SP ceiling chooses two triples before imposing equal
  trace, equal product, decoration, richness, or the quotient-line weight. It
  is a valid ceiling but cannot numerically imply the DSP8 allowance.

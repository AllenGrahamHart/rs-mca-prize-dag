# Audit

- A row-scaling subtrade keeps each selected dual codeword in its original
  block. It is therefore a valid left relation, not merely a coefficient
  dependence detached from the parity stack.
- The upper arity bound is necessary for support minimality. It does not say
  every family with `t<=2r+1` is a circuit.
- During decomposition, a circuit may have lower trade rank. The bound is
  applied again at that actual rank; rank one is excluded by the dependency.
- The Segre atlas is exact for the coefficient equations with fixed `F,G`.
  It does not assert that every abstract Segre packet embeds in the selected
  RS block family.
- Five-point circuits are generic at coefficient level, so attempting to
  exclude all proper rank-two circuits by a determinant identity is false.
- Quotienting the circuit span is structural preprocessing, not by itself a
  payment of the slopes that generated those circuits.

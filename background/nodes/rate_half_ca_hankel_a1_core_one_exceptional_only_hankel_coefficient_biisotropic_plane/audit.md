# Audit

1. Independence of the coefficient vectors uses the proved single minimal
   index `e`, not primitivity alone.
2. The transport identity `(2)` preserves `i+j`; both boundary cases are
   needed to cover the entire coefficient square.
3. `v` is excluded from `W_q` by the proved nonzero `M_1` self-pairing, not
   by a dimension heuristic.
4. The `M_1` radical is one-dimensional because the exceptional root is
   finite in the coordinate `z=E/H`; infinity is an ordinary rank-`2e+1`
   point of the pencil.
5. Maximality is asserted for common isotropy through the `M_1` bound; the
   more degenerate form `M_0` alone can have a larger isotropic subspace.

# Lineage

The quarter-width theorem used only the level-local consequence `p>m^2` of
the official row condition. The exact-ratio tower, however, keeps one ambient
prime while summing every divisor level `m|n`.

This node preserves that ambient prime scale in the same cyclotomic norm
inequality. Since `n` and `m` are powers of two, the resulting logarithmic
threshold simplifies to the exact integer cutoff in `(ALC2)`.

The final strengthening couples the ambient norm to the already-proved
Vandermonde lower bound for nonzero antipodal defect and to the one-side swap
norm when that defect vanishes. This yields `(ALC2-exact)` without a Taylor
approximation.

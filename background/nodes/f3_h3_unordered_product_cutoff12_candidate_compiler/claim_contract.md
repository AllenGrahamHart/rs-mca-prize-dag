# Claim contract

- **claim:** one degree-`n(n-1)/2` unordered-product polynomial, its first
  thirteen Hasse derivatives, and the inverse selector `(T-1)Y=1` give an
  exact scalar candidate support for nonidentity unordered multiplicity at
  least 13; every nonidentity `P>=25` row-prime is retained.
- **scope:** every dyadic `n>=4` and every prime `p=1 mod n`.
- **dependencies:** the integral global product resultant and characteristic-
  zero shifted-product Sidonicity.
- **falsifier:** failure of `Ucal_n^2=Pcal_n Delta_n`, a prime dividing the
  selected scalar without a nonidentity unordered multiplicity-13 root or
  conversely, or a nonidentity product fiber with `P>=25` and `U<=12`.
- **boundary:** `t=1` is removed algebraically. On `t!=1`,
  `U=13,D=2,P=24` is retained and must be rejected by exact row evaluation;
  the scalar support is not claimed to equal the `P>=25` support.
- **nonclaims:** no efficient scalar elimination, official factorization,
  candidate count, resource estimate, maximum-fiber theorem, DSP8 promotion,
  or C36' close is asserted.

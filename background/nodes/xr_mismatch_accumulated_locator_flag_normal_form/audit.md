# Audit

1. Locator layers are disjoint because every next domain is the previous
   discrepancy support. They are not declared disjoint merely because their
   locators multiply.
2. The lifted pair is an original degree-below-`K` pair. The strict degree
   bound uses `deg g_i^j<K_j-d_j`, not a weak non-strict inequality.
3. The global support contains accumulated roots plus a newly truncated local
   support. Earlier non-root agreement coordinates may be lost and are not
   counted again.
4. Zero-size layers are permitted. They change the lifted explanation and
   ambient domain but do not consume locator degree.
5. Prefix divisibility concerns descendant codeword differences. It does not
   say that a final lifted pair differs from the initial pair by the complete
   accumulated locator.
6. The low-core cap applies only to distinct lifted pairs and exact supports
   containing the declared common prefix.
7. The normal form removes overlapping locator-history enumeration but does
   not bound the number of zero-drop transitions, flags, or slopes.
8. No Modal computation or official-row enumeration is used.

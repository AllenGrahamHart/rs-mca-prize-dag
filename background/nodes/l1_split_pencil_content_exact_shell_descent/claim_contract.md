# Claim contract

## Proved content

For every interpolation-module basis and every split complement locator
representing a codeword, the monic gcd of its two basis coefficients is
basis invariant and its degree equals complete agreement minus the displayed
support size.  Coprime coefficients are equivalent to exact-shell ownership,
and division by content is the unique descent to that owner.

## Required hypotheses

- `H` consists of distinct field elements, so `Omega` and every divisor `W`
  are squarefree.
- `g_1,g_2` are an actual `F[X]`-basis of `M_U`.
- `W` is a monic divisor of `Omega` and `N=Wc`.
- The codeword degree condition `deg c<k` is retained for the L1 consumer.

## Not proved

No upper bound on the primitive coefficient-pair census, Q/BC flatness,
quotient transport, reserve absorption, or finite adjacent-row numerator is
claimed.  The result removes raw support multiplicity; it does not pay the
remaining primitive split-pencil points.

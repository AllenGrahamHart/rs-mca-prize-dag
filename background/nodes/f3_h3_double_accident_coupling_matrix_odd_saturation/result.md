# Result

All product, quotient, and cross-lane congruences in the C36' double-accident
ideal are now encoded by one coupling matrix. Away from the dyadic prime, the
traditional joint ideal, one coupling row and column, and the full rectangle
generate the same ideal.

The zero entries form a partial matching, so a rich `U(t) x R(t)` rectangle
has at least `U(t)R(t)-min(U(t),R(t))` nonzero entries. The same theorem proves
these entries are highly redundant as ideal generators. The remaining
arithmetic frontier is a bound for the common odd ideal norm, not a raw
coupling-entry count. No Modal compute was used.

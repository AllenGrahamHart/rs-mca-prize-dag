# Proof

The internal-slice value formula used in antiweight absorption is valid
before any antiweight specialization. If `l in L`, then for every `k!=l`
it gives

```text
c_l(xi_l-xi_k)^2=t_k,                               (1)
```

where `t_k` is nonzero and independent of `l`. If three indices `l` lay in
`L`, choose two official-row indices `k,m` outside those three. Dividing
`(1)` for `k` and `m` would make the squared Mobius function

```text
((X-xi_k)/(X-xi_m))^2
```

take one value at three distinct `xi_l`. A nonconstant degree-two equation
has at most two roots. Thus `|L|<=2`.

First consider the non-antiweight degree-two branch. Every ratio

```text
f_lm=P_lD_m^2/(P_mD_l^2)                            (2)
```

belongs to the common field `F(psi)`, which is the fixed field of `tau`.
Choose one good index `m`, so the roots of `D_m` form a nonfixed
`tau`-orbit. This is possible because the dependency already supplies at
least `e-40` good pairs.

Let `l` be a tail index outside `L` whose pair contains no fixed point. At
least one root `x` of `D_l` has `ord_x(P_l)<=1`. The pair is not a
`tau`-orbit, so `tau(x)` is not the other root of `D_l`. It is not a root of
`D_m` either: invariance of the roots of `D_m` would then put `x` in that
disjoint pair. The pair-crossing nonvanishing condition also gives
`P_m(x)!=0`. Therefore `(2)` has a pole at `x`.

The divisor of `(2)` is `tau`-invariant, so `(2)` has a pole of the same
order at `tau(x)`. At that point neither `D_l` nor `D_m` vanishes. The only
remaining denominator factor is `P_m`, and hence

```text
P_m(tau(x))=0.                                      (3)
```

Choose one such `x` for each tail pair under consideration. Distinct pair
supports give distinct `x`, and the involution is injective, so `(3)`
charges distinct roots of the nonzero quartic `P_m`. There are at most four
of these tail pairs. Adding the at most two indices in `L` and the at most
two pairs containing a fixed point gives six tails in the antipodal case
and eight in the constant-product case.

For completeness, in the antiweight-derived degree-two branch the field is
generated by the nonconstant functions `r_l=P_l/D_l^2`, `l notin L`.
Each such `r_l` is itself `tau`-invariant. If a tail `D_l` outside `L`
contained no fixed point, the same pole argument would have no `P_m`
denominator available: both roots of `D_l` would have to be cancelled to
order two by `P_l`, forcing `P_l` proportional to `D_l^2`, a contradiction.
Thus that branch has at most `|L|` tails plus the number of fixed points,
which is no larger than the displayed uniform bounds.

The bounded-tail row-codegree theorem now substitutes `t<=6` or `t<=8` in
its exact eliminant degree. QED.

# Audit

1. The cap `|L|<=2` uses the exact internal-slice values, not the support
   crossing equations alone.
2. The fixed good index `m` exists with an overwhelming official margin;
   the proof does not choose a new `m` for each tail.
3. At a root of `D_l`, `P_m` is nonzero by the pair-crossing nonvanishing
   condition. At its involution mate, a pole may come from `P_m`; this is
   why the ordinary branch pays four escapes rather than claiming every
   tail is proportional.
4. Distinct tail pairs charge distinct roots because their supports are
   disjoint and `tau` is injective.
5. Fixed points are paid separately. There are none for `x|->-x` on the
   nonzero odd-characteristic subgroup and at most two for `x|->c/x`.
6. In the antiweight-derived branch the invariant function is `r_l` itself,
   so the four-root denominator escape is absent.
7. The theorem only sharpens the degree-two branch. A quartic pullback may
   still have up to 148 normalization tails.

# Result

Nontrivial fully fiberwise dyadic pullbacks cannot occur at exact agreement
`k+ell-1`. Their next quotient agreement is paid by a domain-independent
Johnson bound whenever `h_0^2>bd`, equivalently
`(k+ell)^2>(k-s)n` for `s<=k`; `s>k` is automatic. Across all tame maps in
the fixed source this sector costs at most `n^3`. The remaining full-partition
problem for this theorem is the exact sub-Johnson inequality. The later
full-domain intrinsic-rigidity theorem routes that class to the periodic
owner; partial fibers, sparse coverage, wild fixed-petal decompositions, and
unanchored locators remain.

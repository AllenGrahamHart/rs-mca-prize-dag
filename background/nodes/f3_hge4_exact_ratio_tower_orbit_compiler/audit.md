# Audit

1. The level is generated by all ratios in `P union Q`, not by either support
   separately.
2. Anchoring is essential: only after a root is moved to `1` does the ratio
   subgroup equal the subgroup generated by the roots themselves.
3. Ambient and exact-level primitivity agree because any common stabilizer is
   a ratio of two support elements and hence lies in the ratio subgroup.
4. The exact-level test uses the unique maximal proper dyadic subgroup. It
   would need all maximal proper subgroups at non-prime-power order.
5. The sum includes every dyadic divisor, including small levels with empty
   width ranges. This is what yields `(4n^2-1)/3`.
6. The weighted anchored currency is `C_h/h`; summing raw anchored counts
   would lose the exact orbit normalization.
7. The exact-level and non-full selectors are existential coverage devices.
   Their witness multiplicities must never enter a census.
8. The `21/2` estimate is sufficient, not necessary, and is not promoted to
   a conditional node.

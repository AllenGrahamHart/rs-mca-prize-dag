# Proof

## Exact ratio level

The ratio subgroup in `(ERT1)` is unchanged when both supports are scaled.
Since `mu_n` is cyclic, it is the unique subgroup `mu_m` for some `m|n`.
The union has `2h` distinct elements, so `m>=2h`.

Choose `x in P` and scale the pair by `x^(-1)`. The scaled union contains
`1`, and every one of its elements is a ratio of two original elements.
It therefore lies in `mu_m` and generates `mu_m`. Top-shift equality is
preserved because its degree-`j` elementary symmetric coordinates on both
sides are multiplied by the same scalar `x^(-j)`.

Primitivity is also unchanged by this descent. Indeed, if a scalar `gamma`
stabilizes both scaled supports, then for any `u` in their union, both `u`
and `gamma u` lie in `mu_m`; hence `gamma in mu_m`. Thus the common scaling
stabilizer computed in `mu_n` is the same as the one computed in `mu_m`.

Changing the anchor from `x` to another element of `P` scales the normalized
pair by an element of `mu_m`, so an ambient orbit gives one exact-level
`mu_m` orbit. Conversely, include any exact-level pair in `mu_m` into
`mu_n`. If two such pairs at levels `m` and `m'` are ambient-scaling
equivalent, their ratio subgroups agree, so `m=m'`. If two pairs at that
same level are related by `gamma in mu_n`, choose `u` in the first union.
Then `u,gamma u in mu_m`, whence `gamma in mu_m`; they were already in the
same level orbit. This proves the bijection `(ERT2)`.

The action on primitive ordered pairs is free by the orbit aggregate router.
Exactly the `h` scales `x^(-1)`, for `x in P`, put `1` in the left support.
This proves `(ERT3)`.

## Geometric tower budget

Sum `(ERT2)` over `4<=h<=H_max` and enlarge each inner width range:

```text
sum_h O_h^prim(n,p)
 <=sum_(m|n) sum_(h=4)^floor(m/2) E_h^prim(m,p).     (1)
```

For dyadic `n`,

```text
sum_(m|n)m^2=1+4+...+n^2=(4n^2-1)/3.               (2)
```

Applying `(ERT4)` in `(1)` and then `(2)` gives

```text
sum_h O_h^prim(n,p)
 <=(21/2)(4n^2-1)/3
 =14n^2-7/2
 <14n^2.                                           (3)
```

The orbit aggregate router turns `(3)` into the HGE4 norm-gate bound.
Equation `(ERT6)` follows immediately from `(ERT3)`.

## Exact-level selector

After left anchoring, the ratio subgroup is generated by the roots of `D`.
Every root lies in `mu_m` exactly when `D | X^m-1`. Because `m` is a power
of two, `mu_(m/2)` is the unique maximal proper subgroup of `mu_m`.
Therefore those roots generate all of `mu_m` exactly when at least one root
is outside `mu_(m/2)`, equivalently

```text
D does not divide X^(m/2)-1.                        (4)
```

The orientation `L_P=S-a`, `L_Q=S+a` puts `1` in the left support exactly
when `S(1)-a=0`. The non-full selector is the exact projection theorem from
the non-full near-square lift.

In its repeated-squaring recurrence, `V_t` is the unique remainder of
`X^(2^t)` modulo the monic polynomial `D`. Hence `D | X^m-1` is `V_r=1`,
and `(4)` is `V_(r-1)!=1`. Writing

```text
V_(r-1)-1=sum_(i=0)^(2h-1) b_i X^i,
```

the equation `sum_i w_i b_i=1` has a solution exactly when this remainder is
nonzero. It is therefore an exact existential selector for the final clause
of `(ERT7)`.

Every primitive top shift pair is non-full: a full `mu_h` fiber pair has the
nontrivial common stabilizer `mu_h`. It gives one base pair `(S,a)` with the
stated orientation. Dropping primitivity can only enlarge the base solution
set, proving `(ERT8)`. Selector fibers are not part of either count. QED.
